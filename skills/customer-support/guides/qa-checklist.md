# QA Checklist — Customer Support

- The actual customer issue is identified, not paraphrased vaguely.
- The response leads with the answer, status, or next step.
- Any troubleshooting steps are concrete, ordered, and testable.
- Requested follow-up details are truly necessary.
- Facts, ownership, and timelines are verified or clearly qualified.
- The reply separates known information from investigation in progress.
- Escalations include impact, evidence, prior attempts, and the specific ask.
- Policy explanations are plain-language and non-defensive.
- The customer knows what to do next and when they will hear back.
- The tone is calm, human, and free of empty empathy filler.
