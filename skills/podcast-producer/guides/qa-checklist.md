# QA Checklist — Podcast Producer

Use this before finalizing any podcast-production output.

## 1. Listener promise and format
- Is it clear why someone would subscribe and come back?
- Does the format match the host's strengths and the team's actual capacity?
- Is cadence realistic, not aspirational?

## 2. Workflow completeness
- Are the critical stages covered: planning, booking, prep, recording, editing, publishing, and post-release learning?
- Are owners, handoffs, and deadlines explicit where relevant?
- Are guest steps included when the format depends on guests?

## 3. Upstream discipline
- Does the answer address prep quality and recording reliability, not just editing?
- Are backup / contingency paths named where useful?
- Does it avoid assuming post can rescue weak raw material?

## 4. Sustainability
- Can this process still run during a chaotic month?
- Does it account for batching, backlog, cancellations, and approval lag?
- Is the workflow lightweight enough for the actual team size?

## 5. Packaging and growth
- Are title, show-note, transcript, clip, and distribution choices handled when relevant?
- Does the packaging logic improve discoverability without creating content-sprawl theater?
- Are success metrics or review rhythms practical?

## 6. Output usefulness
- Would a producer know what to do next?
- Are the highest-priority changes obvious?
- Is the output operational rather than generic inspiration?
