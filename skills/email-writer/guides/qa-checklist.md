# QA Checklist — Email Writer

Use this before finalizing any email-writing output.

## 1. Objective clarity
- Is the purpose obvious in the first lines?
- Is the primary ask or message singular and explicit?
- Would the recipient know exactly what to do next?

## 2. Tone fit
- Does the tone match the relationship, power dynamic, and stakes?
- Is the draft human and credible rather than stiff or gushy?
- Have accidental sharp edges, blame, or weak hedging been removed?

## 3. Recipient effort
- Is the email easy to scan?
- Are dates, owners, options, or deadlines clear?
- Has unnecessary backstory been cut?

## 4. Risk control
- Could this be forwarded safely?
- Are sensitive statements precise enough?
- Does the draft avoid manipulative, deceptive, or legally careless wording?

## 5. Deliverable completeness
- Are subject lines included when useful?
- Are alternate variants offered when tone calibration matters?
- If replying to an existing situation, does the email reflect the actual context and thread state?

## 6. Writing quality
- Are sentences concise and readable?
- Are verbs concrete?
- Does the email sound like something a competent person would actually send?
- Does it improve the odds of the desired response?
