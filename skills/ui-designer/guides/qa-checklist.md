# QA Checklist — ui-designer

- The screen goal, user priority, and constraints are explicit.
- The output distinguishes visual-design ownership from flow-design or research ownership.
- Information hierarchy and grouping are concrete, not implied.
- Layout, spacing, density, and component arrangement are specific enough to act on.
- Loading, empty, error, success, disabled, and overflow states are addressed where relevant.
- Responsive behavior across key breakpoints is covered.
- Accessibility concerns such as contrast, focus visibility, readable hierarchy, and touch targets are considered.
- Handoff notes make clear what engineering must preserve versus what is flexible.
- Decorative opinions do not replace problem-solving.
- The result is concise, structured, and implementation-ready.
