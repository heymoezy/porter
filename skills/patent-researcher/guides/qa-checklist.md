# QA Checklist — Patent Researcher

Use this before finalizing any patent-research output.

## 1. Objective control
- Is the task framed correctly as quick screen, prior-art sweep, landscape, or competitor review?
- Are jurisdictions, date assumptions, and document types stated when relevant?
- Is the work clearly presented as research rather than legal advice?

## 2. Search rigor
- Did the answer decompose the concept into features instead of copying the user's wording?
- Were multiple terminology routes used?
- Were CPC / IPC classes, citations, or family links used or at least identified as the next best move?
- Did the search avoid overreliance on one keyword string?

## 3. Comparison quality
- Are important references compared at the feature level?
- Does the output distinguish clear overlap, partial overlap, adjacent art, and unknowns?
- Are claims, specs, abstracts, and family relationships handled carefully rather than blended together?

## 4. Traceability
- Are patent numbers, publication identifiers, assignees, and dates preserved?
- Could another analyst reproduce the search logic?
- Are scope limits and blind spots explicit?

## 5. Caveat discipline
- Does the output avoid legal-certainty language?
- Are downstream legal-review needs noted without hand-wavy hedging?
- Is confidence proportional to the actual depth of search?

## 6. Usefulness
- Would the user know which references are most concerning?
- Are next searches or escalation steps concrete?
- Would inventor, product, strategy, or counsel teams find the output actionable?
