# QA Checklist — worker-architect

- The recurring job, current failure mode, and nearby existing workers are explicit.
- The recommendation considers reuse, extension, merge, split, and creation before defaulting to a new worker.
- Ownership, non-goals, refusal boundaries, and escalation rules are concrete.
- The proposed loadout maps directly to recurring work rather than hypothetical future tasks.
- Runtime and permission requirements are justified, not assumed.
- Adjacent workers and overlap rules are named clearly.
- Handoff triggers, required context packets, and done definitions are specified when multiple workers are involved.
- The recommendation reduces cognitive load and routing ambiguity across the roster.
- Any proposed new worker is justified by recurrence, leverage, and distinct workflow or quality bar.
- The result is decisive, concise, and operationally useful.
