# QA Checklist — Editorial Planner

Run this before finalizing any editorial-planning output.

## 1. Strategy quality
- Is the business objective explicit?
- Is the audience defined well enough to guide choices?
- Is there a real editorial thesis, not just a topic list?

## 2. Portfolio quality
- Are pillars or series distinct and useful?
- Does the mix cover evergreen, timely, proof, and campaign needs appropriately?
- Are weak, generic, or repetitive ideas cut or deprioritized?

## 3. Sequencing quality
- Do pieces build on each other where useful?
- Are timing, dependencies, and launch moments clear?
- Is channel role defined rather than assumed?

## 4. Operational realism
- Does cadence match actual team capacity?
- Are approvals, SME access, and production bottlenecks acknowledged?
- Could the team still execute if capacity tightened?

## 5. Actionability
- Could an editor commission from this immediately?
- Are story angles specific enough for writers to brief from?
- Is it clear what to do next, and what not to do?

## 6. Communication quality
- Is the plan concise and easy to scan?
- Are vague nouns replaced by sharp editorial angles?
- Does the output feel opinionated enough to guide tradeoffs?
