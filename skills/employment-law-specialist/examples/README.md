# Employment Law Specialist — Example Output Shapes

Use these patterns to shape strong employment-law analysis deliverables.

## Example 1 — Termination risk review

**Input:**
Can we terminate this employee next week for performance issues?

**Good output shape:**
- jurisdiction and status assumptions
- key risk drivers
- missing documentation / chronology gaps
- retaliation, leave, accommodation, or discrimination questions to check
- safer options or pause conditions
- explicit counsel-review threshold

## Example 2 — Policy review

**Input:**
Review our attendance policy for employment-law risk.

**Good output shape:**
- main legal pressure points
- ambiguous or overbroad language
- leave / accommodation interactions
- manager-enforcement risks
- recommended revisions or caution notes

## Example 3 — Worker classification

**Input:**
We call these workers contractors. What are the risks?

**Good output shape:**
- jurisdiction caveat
- fact questions driving classification analysis
- likely exposure areas
- contract / practice mismatches
- recommended follow-up review steps

## Example 4 — Harassment complaint handling

**Input:**
An employee reported repeated inappropriate comments. What should HR do now?

**Good output shape:**
- immediate process priorities
- evidence and witness checklist
- interim-measure considerations
- retaliation-risk reminders
- when counsel or specialist investigators should be involved

## Example 5 — Leave / accommodation scenario

**Input:**
A manager wants to deny a remote-work request tied to a medical issue.

**Good output shape:**
- likely legal frameworks implicated
- missing facts needed before decision
- process errors to avoid
- safer interactive steps
- escalation guidance

## Example 6 — Wage issue review

**Input:**
We have salaried employees working long hours. Analyze overtime risk.

**Good output shape:**
- jurisdiction caveat
- exemption / classification questions
- recordkeeping and practice risks
- likely exposure themes
- immediate containment and counsel-review steps
