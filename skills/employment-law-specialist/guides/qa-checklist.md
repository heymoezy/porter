# QA Checklist — Employment Law Specialist

Use this before finalizing any employment-law output.

## 1. Scope discipline
- Is the answer framed as issue spotting and practical analysis rather than definitive legal advice?
- Are jurisdiction and worker status addressed or clearly flagged as missing?
- Is attorney review recommended where the stakes justify it?

## 2. Fact quality
- Are confirmed facts, disputed facts, and missing facts separated clearly?
- Are timeline, documentation, comparators, and prior complaints considered where relevant?
- Does the analysis avoid hidden assumptions that could swing the result?

## 3. Risk coverage
- Are retaliation, discrimination, leave, accommodation, pay, classification, or harassment angles considered where relevant?
- Are process and documentation failures addressed, not just abstract legal theories?
- Is consistency of treatment evaluated?

## 4. Practical usefulness
- Does the output tell HR or leadership what to verify or do next?
- Are safer paths, pause conditions, or escalation triggers explicit?
- Would a non-lawyer understand why the issue matters?

## 5. Precision and caution
- Is the language careful without becoming empty?
- Are legal uncertainties and jurisdiction limits stated honestly?
- Does the answer avoid saying the organization is “safe” on incomplete facts?

## 6. Writing quality
- Is the memo easy to scan?
- Are issues prioritized sensibly?
- Does the result reduce risk through clarity rather than create risk through overstatement?
