# QA Checklist — Speech Writer

Use this before finalizing speech work.

## 1. Occasion and speaker fit
- Is the speaking moment clear?
- Does the language sound believable for this specific speaker?
- Are assumptions explicit where voice, politics, or room mood are uncertain?

## 2. Spoken-language quality
- Does the draft sound natural aloud?
- Are sentence length, transitions, and rhythm manageable under live delivery?
- Are there obvious stumble points, jargon clumps, or fact piles that should be cut?

## 3. Structure and audience impact
- Is there one clear message spine?
- Does the speech open quickly, progress cleanly, and land well?
- Will the audience remember the main beats and any call to action?

## 4. Emotional and situational judgment
- Does the tone match the occasion without sounding flat or theatrical?
- Are sensitive topics handled with enough restraint and specificity?
- Does the work avoid generic inspiration or manipulative applause bait?

## 5. Writing quality
- Is the output concise enough for the requested time window?
- Would a strong speaker or comms lead trust this as delivery-ready material?
- Does it meet the quality bar: speakability, voice fit, audience resonance, structural clarity, emotional control, memorable phrasing?
