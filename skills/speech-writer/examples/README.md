# Speech Writer — Example Output Shapes

Use these patterns to keep speeches sayable, situationally aware, and structurally clean.

## Example 1 — Founder keynote

**Input:**
Write a 7-minute founder keynote that introduces a new product direction, thanks the team, and gives customers a reason to believe.

**Good output shape:**
- occasion and audience framing
- message spine and major beats
- full draft or detailed outline
- lines worth emphasizing
- trim options if time runs short

## Example 2 — Hard-quarter town hall

**Input:**
Rewrite executive remarks for an internal town hall after layoffs and a missed quarter.

**Good output shape:**
- what the room needs emotionally and practically
- tone guardrails
- rewritten remarks in plain spoken language
- lines to slow down or simplify
- risks to avoid sounding evasive or performative

## Example 3 — Ceremonial speech

**Input:**
Draft a commencement speech with warmth, wit, and a real lesson without cliché.

**Good output shape:**
- core theme and audience promise
- opening hook options
- full draft with clean transitions
- memorable line candidates
- notes on what to cut if it starts feeling generic

## Example 4 — Panel talking points

**Input:**
Create talking points for a conference panel so the speaker sounds prepared but unscripted.

**Good output shape:**
- audience and panel dynamic assumptions
- 5 to 8 core points with evidence or examples
- bridge lines and pivots
- optional stories or proof points
- red-flag phrases to avoid

## Example 5 — Crisis or apology address

**Input:**
Draft short remarks after a service failure where the speaker must be accountable without overpromising.

**Good output shape:**
- what must be acknowledged
- what commitments are safe to make
- concise draft for live delivery
- wording to avoid for legal or trust reasons
- follow-up questions that need confirmation
