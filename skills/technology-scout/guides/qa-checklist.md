# QA Checklist — Technology Scout

- The decision to be made is explicit.
- Hard constraints and evaluation criteria are defined.
- Irrelevant options are excluded rather than exhaustively listed.
- Comparisons cover fit, maturity, operational burden, migration effort, cost shape, and lock-in where relevant.
- Evidence quality is distinguished from vendor marketing or speculation.
- Recommendations match the team's capacity, timeline, and risk tolerance.
- The output clearly separates adopt-now candidates, pilot candidates, and watch-list items when useful.
- Risks, caveats, and reversal or exit considerations are visible.
- A concrete next step is included: pilot, benchmark, procurement, adoption, or defer-with-trigger.
- Writing is concise, opinionated, and decision-useful.
