# Press Release Writer — Example Output Shapes

Use these patterns to keep releases news-first and publishable.

## Example 1 — Product launch release

**Input:**
Announce a new B2B analytics product for retailers.

**Good output shape:**
- Headline
- Optional subhead
- Dateline + lead paragraph with who / what / why now
- Details:
  - what the product does
  - availability / pricing / market scope
  - what problem it solves
- Quote from company leader
- Optional customer or partner quote if approved
- Boilerplate + media contact

## Example 2 — Funding announcement

**Input:**
Write a release for a Series A.

**Good output shape:**
- Headline with round type and company name
- Lead with amount, lead investor, and planned use of funds
- Details:
  - traction or product context
  - investor names
  - what the capital enables next
- CEO quote
- Investor quote
- Boilerplate

## Example 3 — Partnership release

**Input:**
Announce a strategic distribution partnership.

**Good output shape:**
| Section | What it should answer |
|---|---|
| Lead | Who partnered, for what, and why it matters |
| Deal detail | product / market / channel scope |
| Customer value | what changes for buyers or users |
| Quotes | perspective from both sides |
| Availability | when and where the partnership goes live |

## Example 4 — Weak-angle rewrite

**Input:**
Our team won an internal award and wants a press release.

**Good output shape:**
- Brief critique: weak external-news value
- Stronger alternatives:
  - internal memo
  - customer newsletter note
  - newsroom short if tied to a larger milestone
- If forced to write, use a narrowly factual company-news format and avoid hype
