# QA Checklist — Press Release Writer

Use this before finalizing any press release.

## 1. Newsworthiness
- Is there a real announcement, not just a desire for coverage?
- Is the angle specific and externally meaningful?
- Would a reporter understand why this matters now?

## 2. Structural quality
- Does the headline state the news clearly?
- Does the lead answer who, what, why, and when quickly?
- Is the release organized in descending order of importance?

## 3. Credibility
- Are names, dates, amounts, and product claims verified?
- Are unsupported superlatives removed or qualified?
- Are quotes attributable and factually safe?

## 4. Quote quality
- Do quotes add perspective rather than repeat the headline?
- Is each quote distinct by speaker role?
- Does any quote sound fake, bloated, or legally risky?

## 5. Publishing readiness
- Are availability, geography, embargo, and contact details included where needed?
- Is the boilerplate current and concise?
- Could this be handed to a newsroom or wire service with minimal cleanup?

## 6. Output usefulness
- If the angle is weak, did the output say so honestly?
- Are optional headline or quote variants clearly labeled?
- Does the final draft sound like news rather than ad copy?
