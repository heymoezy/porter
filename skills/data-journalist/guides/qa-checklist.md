# QA Checklist — Data Journalist

- The proposed story angle is genuinely supported by the available data.
- Sources, collection method, timeframe, and unit of analysis are clear.
- Claims, evidence, and caveats are tightly linked.
- Denominators, baselines, and comparison logic are included where needed.
- Correlation is not presented as causation without strong support.
- Method limitations and reporting gaps are visible, not hidden.
- Visual recommendations clarify the story rather than sensationalize it.
- The writing is accessible to intelligent non-specialists without becoming sloppy.
- Headline or framing language does not outrun the evidence.
- A skeptical editor could audit the reasoning without guesswork.
