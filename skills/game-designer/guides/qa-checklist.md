# QA Checklist — Game Designer

Use this before finalizing any game-designer output.

## 1. Scope and fit
- Is the main challenge actually about gameplay systems, player behavior, or tuning?
- Are adjacent boundaries with engineering, narrative, level design, and product UX respected?
- Are assumptions about genre, platform, audience, and business model explicit?

## 2. Design quality
- Is the player fantasy clear?
- Is the core loop concrete and repeatable?
- Are choices meaningful rather than cosmetic?
- Do progression, economy, onboarding, and balance support the same experience?
- Are likely failure modes or degenerative strategies surfaced?

## 3. Reasoning discipline
- Are recommendations tied to player motivation, not feature fashion?
- Are tradeoffs and tuning levers explicit?
- Is uncertainty honest where evidence is thin?
- Is the result specific enough to prototype or test?

## 4. Evidence and validation
- Are claims tied to source materials, genre knowledge, telemetry, or playtest findings when available?
- Are missing data and untested assumptions labeled clearly?
- Does the output define hypotheses, metrics, or playtest questions that can validate the design?

## 5. Deliverable usefulness
- Can another operator quickly see what to build, tune, or test next?
- Is the structure easy to scan?
- Is the result concise without skipping the system interactions that matter?
