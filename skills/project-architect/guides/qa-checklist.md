# QA Checklist — Project Architect

Use this before finalizing any project-architecture output.

## 1. Objective discipline
- Is there one primary objective?
- Are assumptions visible instead of buried?
- Has adjacent-but-separate work been split out?

## 2. Scope control
- Are in-scope, out-of-scope, and later-phase items explicit?
- Is phase 1 small enough to start credibly?
- Has “everything is urgent” been resisted?

## 3. Sequencing realism
- Does the order reflect real dependencies and decision gates?
- Are critical unknowns pulled early?
- Are parallel lanes used only where they are truly safe?

## 4. Workstream clarity
- Are owners or owner types clear?
- Are interfaces and handoffs visible?
- Are cross-functional approvals or reviews accounted for?

## 5. Risk realism
- Are blockers and fragile assumptions named directly?
- Are mitigation or validation steps included for major risks?
- Is timeline certainty avoided when evidence is weak?

## 6. Output usefulness
- Could a team start from this plan tomorrow?
- Is there a short list of immediate next actions?
- Does the plan reduce confusion instead of adding project theater?
