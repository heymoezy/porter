# QA Checklist — training-developer

- The learner audience, baseline, and real job context are explicit.
- The performance gap and target outcomes are clearly defined.
- Learning objectives are observable and measurable.
- The chosen format fits the stakes, timing, and environment.
- Content sequencing supports comprehension before independent execution.
- Practice activities mirror real work instead of abstract theory.
- Assessment checks actual readiness, not just content exposure.
- Facilitation, rollout, and reinforcement needs are addressed.
- The program avoids bloat and focuses on the few capabilities that matter most.
- The output is concrete enough for a team to run, review, or adapt.
