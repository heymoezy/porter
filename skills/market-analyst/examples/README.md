# Market Analyst — Example Output Shapes

Use these as patterns for decision-ready market work.

## Example 1 — TAM / SAM / SOM memo

**Input:**
Estimate the market for AI note-taking tools used by North American B2B sales teams.

**Good output shape:**
- decision to support
- market definition and exclusions
- top-down estimate
- bottom-up estimate
- sensitivity table for seat count, adoption, and price
- final range with confidence level
- implication for GTM focus

## Example 2 — Segment prioritization

**Input:**
Which SMB segment should a payroll startup target first?

**Good output shape:**
| Segment | Pain intensity | Willingness to pay | CAC complexity | Compliance burden | Competitive crowding | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| restaurants | high | medium | medium | low | high | 2 |
| home health | high | high | medium | high | medium | 1 |

Then add:
- why the top segment wins
- what assumptions matter most
- what proof to gather next before committing spend

## Example 3 — Market-entry recommendation

**Input:**
Should we expand a vertical SaaS product from the UK into Germany or Benelux first?

**Good output shape:**
- market definition and comparable buying units
- geography comparison table
- demand and channel differences
- regulatory / localization friction
- likely wedge by geography
- recommendation with confidence and first validation step

## Example 4 — Competitive landscape

**Input:**
Map the project-management market around agency workflows.

**Good output shape:**
- category clusters
- direct vs substitute competitors
- each cluster’s wedge and weakness
- where value accrues today
- whitespace opportunities
- strategic implication for positioning and product scope
