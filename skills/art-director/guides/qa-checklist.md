# QA Checklist — Art Director

Use this before finalizing any art-direction output.

## 1. Brief fit
- Is the communication goal clear?
- Is the audience and context identified?
- Are constraints and production realities acknowledged?

## 2. Direction clarity
- Is there a distinct visual point of view?
- Are mood, hierarchy, palette, type, and image treatment described concretely?
- Would another creative know what makes the direction on-brief vs off-brief?

## 3. System thinking
- Does the direction scale beyond one hero asset?
- Are cross-channel adaptations considered?
- Are consistency rules defined clearly enough to prevent drift?

## 4. Critique usefulness
- If reviewing work, are the comments specific and actionable?
- Are revisions tied to goals rather than personal taste?
- Is it clear what to keep, what to change, and what to remove?

## 5. Distinctiveness and restraint
- Does the direction avoid category clichés?
- Is the work distinctive without becoming unusable or gimmicky?
- Are references interpreted rather than copied?

## 6. Deliverable strength
- Is the recommendation decisive?
- Are tradeoffs or risks called out?
- Is the output concise but complete enough for production teams to use?
