# Art Director — Example Output Shapes

Use these as patterns for strong art-direction deliverables.

## Example 1 — Campaign visual territory

**Input:**
We need art direction for a launch campaign for an AI productivity product aimed at operators, not consumers.

**Good output shape:**
- brief summary: audience, promise, channel mix
- 2–3 visual territories with names
- for each territory: mood, palette, type style, image treatment, layout behavior
- recommendation with rationale
- what to avoid because it feels too consumer-tech or too generic SaaS
- downstream guidance for landing page, social, and presentation assets

## Example 2 — Critique of existing direction

**Input:**
Review these current homepage concepts and tell us which one to pursue.

**Good output shape:**
- strongest concept and why
- what each concept signals emotionally
- hierarchy and clarity issues
- brand-fit concerns
- exact revisions for the chosen route
- what to cut rather than refine

## Example 3 — Direction for a multi-asset system

**Input:**
Set art direction for our Q3 reports, social promos, and keynote deck.

**Good output shape:**
- unifying visual principles
- typography and grid guidance
- color and chart treatment rules
- image/illustration system notes
- per-channel adaptation notes
- consistency risks to monitor as assets roll out

## Example 4 — Production brief for image makers

**Input:**
Create a direction brief for the illustrator and photographer on our rebrand campaign.

**Good output shape:**
- campaign feeling and brand traits to express
- visual references with interpretation
- subject, environment, lighting, and composition guidance
- texture/stylization rules
- examples of on-brief vs off-brief choices
- approval criteria before final delivery

## Example 5 — Direction correction memo

**Input:**
Our visuals keep feeling cluttered and inconsistent. Fix the direction.

**Good output shape:**
- diagnosis of current failure modes
- principle changes: simplify, intensify, standardize, reframe
- updated visual rules
- before/after descriptions
- immediate next-step fixes by asset type
- guardrails for future reviews
