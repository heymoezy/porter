# QA Checklist — Delegation Governor

Use this before finalizing any delegation-governance answer.

## 1. Necessity
- Did you explicitly decide whether delegation is needed?
- Would direct execution be simpler, faster, or safer?
- Did you avoid splitting trivial work?

## 2. Boundary quality
- Are subtasks meaningfully bounded?
- Is each subtask large enough to justify a handoff?
- Are dependencies between subtasks manageable?

## 3. Handoff quality
- Does each delegated unit include scope and exclusions?
- Are required inputs, tools, or files named?
- Is the return format explicit?
- Is the quality bar clear?

## 4. Context discipline
- Are you passing only the necessary context?
- Did you protect sensitive or privileged information?
- Did you avoid assuming delegates know hidden background?

## 5. Mergeability
- Is there a concrete merge plan?
- Is it clear who resolves conflicts?
- Are parallel versus sequential choices justified?

## 6. Accountability
- Is final ownership explicit?
- Did you preserve central judgment where needed?
- Did you avoid using delegation to dodge responsibility?
