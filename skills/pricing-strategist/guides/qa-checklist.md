# QA Checklist — Pricing Strategist

Use this before finalizing any pricing recommendation.

## 1. Problem clarity
- Is the pricing problem clearly stated?
- Is the decision tied to conversion, expansion, retention, margin, or deal quality?
- Are key assumptions visible?

## 2. Value and segmentation
- Does the output identify who buys and who uses?
- Is the value metric explained and justified?
- Are segment differences reflected in the recommendation?

## 3. Commercial realism
- Does the recommendation account for margin, support, and billing complexity?
- Is the sales motion reflected in packaging and contract logic?
- Are procurement or enterprise deal realities considered where relevant?

## 4. Tradeoffs and risk
- Are upside, downside, and likely objections explicit?
- Does the output flag cannibalization, churn, or discount-sprawl risk?
- Is competitor data used as context rather than as the whole case?

## 5. Implementation quality
- Could sales, product, finance, and revops operationalize this?
- Are tiers, limits, add-ons, or approval thresholds concrete enough to use?
- Is there a rollout or experiment path when certainty is low?

## 6. Output usefulness
- Does the recommendation sound defensible, not arbitrary?
- Is there a clear preferred direction?
- Would the reader know what to test or decide next?
