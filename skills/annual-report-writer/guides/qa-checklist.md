# QA Checklist — Annual Report Writer

Use this before finalizing any annual-report output.

## 1. Audience and purpose
- Is the primary audience explicit?
- Does the structure fit the organization type and reporting goal?
- Is the tone appropriate for stakeholders rather than generic marketing?

## 2. Narrative quality
- Is there a coherent through-line for the year?
- Are the most important wins, setbacks, and changes surfaced clearly?
- Does the report sound specific to the organization rather than templated?

## 3. Evidence quality
- Are major claims tied to metrics, examples, or verified facts?
- Are uncertain or missing data points flagged instead of guessed?
- Are KPI references consistent across sections?

## 4. Transparency and credibility
- Does the report acknowledge challenges or tradeoffs honestly?
- Does it avoid corporate filler and vague self-praise?
- Are risk/governance/outlook elements addressed where relevant?

## 5. Structure and usability
- Do sections work independently for skimming readers?
- Is the progression logical from reflection to performance to outlook?
- Could a design/layout team use this structure cleanly?

## 6. Output usefulness
- Would leadership recognize this as accurate and usable?
- Would stakeholders learn something real from it?
- Is the output concise but complete?
