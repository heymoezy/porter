# QA Checklist — Style Guide Writer

Use this before finalizing.

## 1. Scope and fit
- Is it clear what teams, channels, and content types the guide covers?
- Are source materials or assumptions stated honestly?
- Is the main inconsistency problem clearly identified?

## 2. Rule quality
- Are principles translated into concrete rules?
- Are constants separated from flex points?
- Are required terms, banned terms, and naming logic explicit?

## 3. Example strength
- Do examples show real contrast between preferred and non-preferred writing?
- Could a writer imitate the examples correctly?
- Do examples actually obey the written rules?

## 4. Adoption usefulness
- Could a new writer use this without live interpretation?
- Could an editor review consistently from it?
- Is the guide concise and scannable enough to survive real use?

## 5. Honesty and completeness
- Are unresolved brand or compliance gaps surfaced instead of papered over?
- Did the work avoid adjective soup, filler, and contradiction?
- Does it meet the quality bar: enforceable, adoptable, example-rich, context-aware, distinctive?
