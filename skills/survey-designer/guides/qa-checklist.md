# QA Checklist — Survey Designer

Use this before finalizing.

## 1. Brief fit
- Is the decision this survey must support explicit?
- Is the target respondent defined clearly enough to write answerable questions?
- Are assumptions, tracker constraints, and required stakeholder asks visible?

## 2. Measurement quality
- Does each question measure one thing clearly?
- Are wording, recall windows, and concepts realistic for respondents?
- Are scales balanced, labeled appropriately, and directionally consistent?
- Do answer choices avoid overlap, omission, and hidden assumptions?

## 3. Burden and flow
- Is the survey as short as it can reasonably be?
- Are screeners early, sensitive items later, and branches reducing irrelevance?
- Did the design avoid long grids, repetition, and mobile-hostile formatting?

## 4. Analysis readiness
- Will the data support the intended cuts, segments, and decisions?
- Are “other,” “none,” “not applicable,” and “don’t know” options handled intentionally?
- Would another researcher understand how to code and interpret the answers?

## 5. Credibility
- Did the work avoid drifting into sample procurement, qualitative moderation, or stats-only work?
- Did it avoid fake psychometric claims and unsupported certainty?
- Is the final result strong on: validity, neutrality, burden control, and field-ready clarity?
