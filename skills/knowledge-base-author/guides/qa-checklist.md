# QA Checklist — Knowledge Base Author

- Title matches the user’s task wording, not internal team jargon.
- Reader, scope, and success state are obvious in the opening.
- Prerequisites, permissions, and dependencies are explicit.
- Steps are ordered, testable, and use exact labels or observable system states.
- Troubleshooting covers likely failure paths, not just the happy path.
- Escalation threshold is clear when self-service should stop.
- Duplicate or conflicting guidance has been removed or flagged.
- Metadata suggestions improve findability: summary, tags, related articles, owner, review date.
- Claims stay within verified evidence; unknowns are labeled.
- Output is concise enough to scan fast during real work.
