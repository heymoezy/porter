# QA Checklist — Animation Lead

Use this before finalizing any animation-lead output.

## 1. Stage correctness
- Is the production stage explicit?
- Are the notes appropriate for that stage?
- Does the output avoid giving polish feedback before blocking/readability issues are resolved?

## 2. Communication clarity
- Is the animation goal clear?
- Do the notes improve what the viewer should understand or feel?
- Are readability and staging addressed?

## 3. Craft quality
- Are timing and spacing discussed when relevant?
- Are pose clarity, silhouette, arcs, anticipation, or overlap addressed usefully?
- Are notes tied to actual animation behavior rather than vague taste?

## 4. Review usefulness
- Are blockers separated from polish?
- Are notes specific enough to execute?
- Are shot/beat references clear?

## 5. Production realism
- Are runtime, platform, frame rate, or render constraints considered when relevant?
- Does the guidance respect asset/pipeline realities?
- Are sequence continuity and handoff concerns covered if needed?

## 6. Output quality
- Would an animator, motion designer, or producer know what to do next?
- Is the feedback concise but complete?
- Does the result move the work closer to approval?
