# QA Checklist — Customer Success Manager

- The desired customer outcome is explicit.
- The analysis uses leading indicators, not just lagging churn or renewal signals.
- Root cause is identified instead of symptom-labeling.
- Stakeholder coverage, champion strength, and executive sponsorship are considered.
- Recommended actions have owners, timing, and success criteria.
- Health-score suggestions are operationally meaningful and tied to owner response.
- Renewal and expansion guidance is grounded in demonstrated value.
- Risks are stated plainly and prioritized.
- The output is immediately usable by a CSM or account team.
- Support work is not being confused with success strategy.
