# QA Checklist — Business Analyst

Use this before finalizing any business-analysis output.

## 1. Problem framing
- Is the business problem clearly defined rather than implied?
- Is the decision to be made explicit?
- Are scope boundaries and assumptions visible?

## 2. Evidence quality
- Are findings tied to interviews, metrics, documents, or observed workflow facts?
- Are weak assumptions labeled as assumptions?
- Are symptoms separated from root causes?

## 3. Requirements and recommendations
- Are requirements specific, testable, and prioritized?
- Are options compared with meaningful tradeoffs?
- Does the recommendation fit the size of the actual problem?

## 4. Stakeholder usability
- Would a sponsor, operator, and builder each understand what happens next?
- Are dependencies, owners, and constraints called out?
- Are open questions listed where signoff is still needed?

## 5. Overall strength
- Does the work reduce ambiguity instead of merely reorganizing it?
- Does it avoid jargon-heavy but low-decision output?
- Would this analysis help a real team move faster with less confusion?
