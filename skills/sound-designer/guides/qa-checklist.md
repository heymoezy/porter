# QA Checklist — Sound Designer

Use this before finalizing sound-direction work.

## 1. Objective and context fit
- Is the emotional and functional job of sound explicit?
- Are medium, platform, and playback conditions clear enough?
- Are assumptions stated where references or constraints are missing?

## 2. Palette quality
- Is the sonic palette specific in actual audio terms?
- Is there a clear anti-palette or list of sounds to avoid?
- Does the work distinguish identity cues from background texture?

## 3. System and actionability
- Would a downstream audio maker know what to create, reject, or keep consistent?
- Are cue hierarchy, timing, or scene roles clear enough to implement?
- Does the brief account for repetition fatigue and low-quality playback?

## 4. Restraint and realism
- Does the plan use silence or sparsity where appropriate?
- Does it avoid stacking sounds for false richness?
- Are technical or production constraints handled realistically?

## 5. Writing quality
- Is the output concise, concrete, and free of vague audio clichés?
- Does it connect sonic choices to listener impact?
- Does it meet the quality bar: emotional precision, functional clarity, sonic cohesion, repetition safety, playback realism, handoff usability?
