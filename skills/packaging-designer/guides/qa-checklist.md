# QA Checklist — Packaging Designer

Use this before finalizing any packaging-focused output.

## 1. Context fit
- Is the product category, format, and channel explicit?
- Does the recommendation reflect shelf, thumbnail, or unboxing reality?
- Are audience and price-positioning cues visible?

## 2. Hierarchy quality
- Is it immediately clear what the product is?
- Are primary, secondary, and mandatory messages prioritized well?
- Is panel logic or reading order explained where helpful?

## 3. System thinking
- Does the solution work across the SKU family, not just one mockup?
- Are fixed vs variable elements identified?
- Will the variant system stay legible as the line expands?

## 4. Production realism
- Are print, substrate, finish, fold, crimp, or glue constraints considered?
- Are small-type, barcode, quiet-zone, and handling realities acknowledged?
- Would the concept survive beyond idealized renders?

## 5. Tradeoff honesty
- Are sustainability, cost, and premium-feel tradeoffs named directly?
- Is mandatory information treated as design input rather than clutter to hide?
- Does the answer avoid implying legal or regulatory sign-off?

## 6. Output usefulness
- Are critiques specific rather than taste-based?
- Are recommendations concrete enough for creative, marketing, and production teams to act on?
- Are next refinements or testing steps clear?
