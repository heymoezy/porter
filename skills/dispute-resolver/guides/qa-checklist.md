# QA Checklist — Dispute Resolver

Use this before finalizing any dispute-resolution output.

## 1. Issue clarity
- Did you separate agreed facts, disputed facts, allegations, and unknowns?
- Are the parties, stakes, and governing frame clear?
- Did you avoid letting chronology or emotion hide the real decision points?

## 2. Neutrality with honesty
- Is the tone calm and non-inflammatory?
- Did you avoid false equivalence where evidence is uneven?
- Are uncertainty, weak evidence, or missing documentation visible?

## 3. Interest awareness
- Did you distinguish stated positions from underlying interests?
- Are non-negotiables, leverage points, and relationship value identified when relevant?
- Do the options address what each side likely needs, not only what they initially demanded?

## 4. Resolution quality
- Are there multiple realistic options with tradeoffs?
- Is any recommended path justified clearly?
- Did you identify when formal legal, HR, compliance, or management review is required?

## 5. Agreement precision
- If drafting terms, are obligations, deadlines, conditions, and completion criteria explicit?
- Is full resolution distinguished from partial or interim resolution?
- Are follow-up, breach handling, and escalation steps stated where needed?

## 6. Practical usefulness
- Could the output support a real mediation, negotiation, or follow-up meeting?
- Does it reduce confusion and emotional heat?
- Would the next owner know exactly what to do from this output?
