# Dispute Resolver — Example Output Shapes

Use these as patterns for dispute-resolution deliverables.

## Example 1 — Client scope and payment dispute

**Input:**
A client says we missed deliverables; our team says they changed scope without approval. Help structure resolution.

**Good output shape:**
- neutral summary of the dispute
- agreed facts vs disputed facts
- contract/process checkpoints that matter
- each side’s positions and likely interests
- resolution options:
  - revised scope and milestones
  - partial refund or credit
  - acceptance criteria reset
- recommended mediation agenda
- draft agreement points

## Example 2 — Internal team conflict

**Input:**
Two team leads are fighting over ownership and it is blocking a launch.

**Good output shape:**
- issue map by decision area
- agreed operational facts
- authority gaps and dependency conflicts
- interests and non-negotiables
- interim operating agreement
- escalation decision that leadership must make

## Example 3 — Vendor settlement outline

**Input:**
Draft a settlement structure for a vendor payment dispute.

**Good output shape:**
- parties and issue summary
- invoice / deliverable / acceptance-status table
- settlement options with tradeoffs
- draft payment milestones and conditions
- review points for counsel
- breach and follow-up handling

## Example 4 — Partial-resolution memo

**Input:**
We cannot resolve everything today. Summarize where we landed.

**Good output shape:**
- agreed points
- unresolved issues
- evidence still needed
- interim obligations
- date, owner, and trigger for next review
- escalation path if deadlines slip

## Example 5 — Customer complaint de-escalation brief

**Input:**
Help me prepare for a tense customer resolution call.

**Good output shape:**
- likely pressure points and facts to confirm
- neutral framing to open the call
- questions that reveal interests and constraints
- option set to discuss in order
- phrases to avoid
- next-step agreement template
