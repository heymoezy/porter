# QA Checklist — Content Writer

Use this before finalizing any draft.

## 1. Audience and goal
- Is the intended audience clear in the writing choices?
- Does the draft serve a specific reader outcome?
- Is the channel or format respected?

## 2. Structure and readability
- Does the piece have a clear opening, flow, and ending?
- Are headings, sections, or bullets used where they improve scanability?
- Did you remove filler, repetition, and throat-clearing?

## 3. Substance and accuracy
- Are claims grounded in provided or verified facts?
- Are missing specifics surfaced instead of invented?
- Does the draft include enough detail to be useful?

## 4. Boundary discipline
- Is this still general content writing, not deep conversion copy, legal review, or SEO strategy masquerading as content?
- If the task drifted into a more specialized area, is that visible?
- Did you avoid returning brainstorm fragments instead of a draft?

## 5. Output quality
- Would a reviewer consider this a real working draft?
- Is the tone appropriate and consistent?
- Is the output concise without feeling unfinished?
