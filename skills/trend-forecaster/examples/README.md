# Examples — trend-forecaster

## Representative requests

### 1. Category evolution brief
“Forecast how AI shopping agents could reshape B2C checkout over the next 18 months.”

Expected shape:
- define the scope, buyer segment, and decision context
- identify signals across product launches, merchant behavior, payments, and regulation
- separate likely near-term shifts from speculative long-term changes
- end with implications for product, GTM, and measurement

### 2. Weak signals to scenarios
“Turn ten weak signals in creator commerce into three plausible trends for a startup studio.”

Expected shape:
- cluster signals into themes
- explain the mechanism behind each possible trend
- assign confidence and time horizon
- show bets to test now and metrics to monitor

### 3. Durable shift or hype test
“Assess whether sustainability packaging changes are durable demand shifts or mostly brand theater.”

Expected shape:
- compare regulatory, cost, consumer, and supply-chain drivers
- identify what would make the shift persist
- show regional or sector differences
- recommend watchpoints and strategic responses

## Output expectation
A strong answer should:
- use evidence from multiple signal types
- make the future legible without pretending certainty
- connect each pattern to concrete implications and actions
- leave the reader with a watchlist, not just a narrative
