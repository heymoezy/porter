# QA Checklist — 3D Artist

Use this before finalizing any 3D-artist response or deliverable.

## 1. Task fit
- Is the task actually 3D-first, not better handled by a 2D, animation, or product skill?
- Is the target medium explicit: real-time, offline render, concept, or handoff?
- Are assumptions stated if constraints were missing?

## 2. Modeling quality
- Are silhouette and primary forms addressed before micro-detail?
- Are topology comments specific and production-relevant?
- If animation is possible, are deformation areas called out?
- Are scale, pivot, orientation, and transform concerns covered if handoff is involved?

## 3. UV and texture quality
- Are seams, texel density, and stretching discussed when relevant?
- Are texture sets/maps named clearly?
- Is the recommendation coherent with the target platform and fidelity?
- Does the advice avoid unnecessary texture complexity?

## 4. Materials and lookdev
- Are materials physically plausible or intentionally stylized?
- Are roughness, metallicity, and breakup discussed in a useful way?
- Is the material direction tied to the brief instead of random decoration?

## 5. Lighting and rendering
- Does the lighting improve readability of shape and surface?
- Are camera/focal-length choices intentional?
- Are composition/background/shadow decisions mentioned when relevant?
- Is the render setup matched to the goal: marketing, turntable, review, or final frame?

## 6. Production realism
- Are file formats, source files, and export files specified when needed?
- Are naming/versioning/handoff details concrete?
- Are recommendations practical for the stated toolchain?
- Is there a clear distinction between blockers and polish?

## 7. Output quality
- Is the answer concrete rather than generic?
- Would a real 3D artist or producer be able to act on it immediately?
- Are risks, tradeoffs, and next steps explicit?
- Is the result concise but complete?
