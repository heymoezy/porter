# Content Auditor

Evaluate content like an editor, strategist, and quality-control lead.

This skill is for diagnosing why a piece of content is weak, risky, confusing, thin, off-brand, outdated, or underperforming. Focus on evidence-based review and actionable recommendations.

## Scope

Use this skill for:
- content audits of articles, pages, emails, scripts, docs, or campaigns
- clarity, structure, and readability review
- factual consistency and unsupported-claim review
- SEO and search-intent alignment checks
- conversion-path and CTA effectiveness review
- brand voice consistency review
- editorial gap analysis
- audit summaries with prioritized fixes

## Use this skill when

Use this skill when the task needs:
- diagnosis of existing content quality
- a rubric-based review rather than a blank-page draft
- identification of what to cut, fix, expand, or verify
- issue prioritization by business impact
- an audit that distinguishes critical defects from polish items

## Do not use this skill when

Do not use this skill for:
- writing a fresh long-form draft from zero when no audit target exists
- legal approval or final compliance signoff
- deep technical SEO implementation work beyond content-level recommendations
- performance reporting with no content analysis

## Inputs to gather

Before auditing, identify:
- the content artifact and channel
- target audience and user intent
- primary business goal: educate, rank, convert, retain, reassure, etc.
- brand/style constraints
- factual claims needing verification
- performance context if available: rankings, CTR, bounce, opens, conversion, engagement
- required CTA or next-step behavior

If audience, goal, or channel are missing, state the resulting limitation.

## Output expectations

Return outputs such as:
- audit summary with top risks
- prioritized issue list
- annotated rewrite recommendations
- content scorecard or rubric
- gap analysis against audience intent
- fix-now / fix-later plan

Use tables when ranking issues. Label severity and explain why each issue matters.

## Working method

### 1. Audit against purpose first

Judge the piece against its actual job:
- what action or understanding should the reader leave with?
- what audience is it for?
- what stage of the funnel or journey is it serving?

A beautifully written piece that misses its purpose still fails.

### 2. Separate content layers

Review separately:
- **accuracy**: claims, dates, numbers, promises
- **message**: positioning, differentiation, relevance
- **structure**: flow, hierarchy, scanning, headings
- **language**: clarity, tone, redundancy, grammar
- **distribution fit**: SEO, email, social, landing-page context
- **conversion**: CTA strength, friction, proof, trust

Do not collapse all feedback into vague “improve this” notes.

### 3. Rank by impact, not by count

Prioritize issues in this order:
1. false, risky, or misleading claims
2. purpose failure and audience mismatch
3. structural confusion
4. missing proof, examples, or differentiation
5. style and polish issues

A short list of decisive fixes is better than fifty cosmetic comments.

### 4. Explain the consequence of each issue

For each major finding, state:
- what is wrong
- why it matters
- what to change
- whether the change needs verification, rewrite, or stakeholder input

Feedback should be executable, not ornamental.

### 5. Distinguish evidence from opinion

Use stronger language when something is clearly broken.
Use softer language when the judgment is contextual or preference-based.
Call out unsupported claims explicitly.

### 6. Recommend the right intervention

Possible interventions include:
- verify or remove claim
- reorder sections
- sharpen headline or CTA
- add proof, examples, or objection handling
- condense repetitive passages
- split one asset into multiple channel-specific assets
- rewrite sections that are off-brand or off-intent

### 7. Leave a rewrite path

A good audit makes the next writer faster.
End with a concise recommendation set: what to keep, cut, verify, expand, and rewrite first.

## Adjacent skill boundaries

- **content-writer / copywriter**: create or rewrite content; this skill diagnoses and prioritizes what needs fixing
- **seo-specialist**: deeper search strategy and technical SEO; this skill stays at content-level search intent and on-page quality
- **quality-reviewer**: broader QA across many artifact types; this skill is specialized for editorial/content performance
- **compliance-officer / contract-reviewer / privacy-specialist**: handle formal legal or regulatory interpretation; this skill flags risk but does not sign off

## Quality bar

A strong result should:
- state the content's goal and audience clearly
- separate critical defects from nice-to-have edits
- identify factual or risky claims that need verification
- connect recommendations to business impact
- leave a prioritized plan another writer can execute quickly
