# QA Checklist — Content Auditor

Use this before finalizing any content audit.

## 1. Scope and purpose
- Is the content type, audience, and goal explicit?
- Did the audit assess the piece against its real job, not generic writing ideals?
- Are assumptions visible where context is missing?

## 2. Issue quality
- Are critical issues separated from nice-to-have edits?
- Are factual, risky, or misleading claims flagged clearly?
- Does each major issue explain why it matters?

## 3. Actionability
- Could another writer execute the recommendations without guessing?
- Is the fix order prioritized sensibly?
- Are rewrite, verification, and stakeholder-input needs distinguished?

## 4. Boundary discipline
- Does the audit avoid pretending to be formal legal/compliance signoff?
- Are SEO notes limited to content-level recommendations unless deeper work was requested?
- Is the output focused on auditing existing content rather than generating a replacement draft?

## 5. Output quality
- Is the result concise, structured, and decision-useful?
- Would an editor or marketer trust it as a working audit brief?
