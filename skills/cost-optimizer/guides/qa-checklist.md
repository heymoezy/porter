# QA Checklist — Cost Optimizer

Use this before finalizing any cost analysis.

## 1. Visibility and evidence
- Did you identify the biggest cost drivers clearly?
- Are findings tied to actual usage, allocation, or spend evidence?
- Did you distinguish directional assumptions from verified facts?

## 2. Recommendation quality
- Are savings opportunities ranked, not dumped flatly?
- Did you separate quick wins from structural changes?
- Are savings estimates bounded with assumptions?

## 3. Tradeoff discipline
- Did you note performance, reliability, security, or delivery risks?
- Did you avoid framing justified capacity as obvious waste?
- Is there a validation or rollback path where changes are risky?

## 4. Business usefulness
- Does the output connect spend to business activity or unit economics where possible?
- Are ownership and next steps clear?
- Would a team know what to do first after reading this?

## 5. Boundary discipline
- Is this still cost optimization rather than accounting, legal review, or generic architecture advice?
- If vendor/contract issues dominate, is that visible?
- Did you avoid “cut everything” logic masquerading as optimization?
