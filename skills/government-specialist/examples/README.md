# Government Specialist — Example Output Shapes

Use these as patterns for strong public-sector deliverables.

## Example 1 — RFP bid / no-bid memo

**Input:**
Review this county RFP and tell us whether we should bid.

**Good output shape:**
- fit snapshot
- hard eligibility and submission gates
- scored criteria and likely strengths / weaknesses
- delivery, compliance, and teaming risks
- bid / no-bid recommendation with rationale

## Example 2 — Requirement matrix

**Input:**
Turn this solicitation into a team checklist.

**Good output shape:**
- requirement by section or clause
- mandatory vs scored vs optional label
- required artifact or evidence
- owner and dependency suggestion
- deadline or submission mechanic
- disqualification notes

## Example 3 — Policy translation

**Input:**
Explain what this agency guidance changes for our workflow.

**Good output shape:**
- plain-language summary
- operational changes required now
- teams or roles affected
- ambiguities needing confirmation
- suggested follow-up questions for counsel or contracting staff

## Example 4 — Public-sector market entry readiness

**Input:**
What do we need before selling into federal agencies?

**Good output shape:**
- likely registrations, certifications, and readiness prerequisites
- common buying pathways and where they fit
- capability, compliance, and past-performance gaps
- practical sequencing plan
- cautions on agency- and vehicle-specific confirmation
