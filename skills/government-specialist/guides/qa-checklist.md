# QA Checklist — Government Specialist

Use this before finalizing any public-sector output.

## 1. Source discipline
- Did you name the governing source document and work from it?
- Did you distinguish primary-source facts from your interpretation?

## 2. Requirement clarity
- Are eligibility gates, deadlines, submission mechanics, and disqualifiers obvious?
- Are mandatory requirements separated from scored criteria and preferences?

## 3. Operational usefulness
- Can the reader tell what must be done, in what order, and with what evidence?
- Did you convert formal language into concrete actions and owners?

## 4. Risk and restraint
- Are ambiguities, jurisdiction limits, or legal-advice boundaries flagged clearly?
- Did you avoid unsupported claims about eligibility, compliance, or award likelihood?

## 5. Finish quality
- Is the output structured for a high-accountability environment?
- Does it reduce procedural risk and decision fog rather than add more?
