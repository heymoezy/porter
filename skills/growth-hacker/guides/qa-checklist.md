# QA Checklist — Growth Hacker

Use this before finalizing any growth output.

## 1. Bottleneck focus
- Did you identify the highest-leverage funnel constraint?
- Did you avoid spreading effort across too many stages at once?

## 2. Experiment quality
- Is each idea a real hypothesis with a segment, behavior change, and measurable success condition?
- Are test surfaces, timing, and guardrails clear enough to run?

## 3. Prioritization quality
- Is there an explicit ranking by impact, confidence, effort, speed to learn, or risk?
- Did you avoid brainstorm bloat with no decision support?

## 4. Measurement and ethics
- Are primary metrics, guardrails, and instrumentation gaps named?
- Did you avoid dark patterns, misleading incentives, or trust-eroding tactics?

## 5. Finish quality
- Is the plan practical for the team's actual resources?
- Will the team learn something actionable even if the first test loses?
