# QA Checklist — Compliance Officer

Use this before finalizing any compliance-focused output.

## 1. Scope and jurisdiction
- Is the jurisdiction or regulatory scope explicit?
- Are assumptions visible where facts are incomplete?
- Is the obligation area tied to a specific business process?

## 2. Risk and control quality
- Are risks ranked meaningfully?
- Are controls, gaps, and evidence separated clearly?
- Does the analysis move beyond generic compliance language into operational reality?

## 3. Practical usefulness
- Would an operator know what control or remediation action to take next?
- Are ownership, monitoring, and evidence expectations clear enough to act on?
- Is there a realistic remediation path rather than abstract caution?

## 4. Legal boundary discipline
- Is the output careful not to present itself as formal legal advice?
- Are interpretation-heavy or high-exposure issues flagged for specialist review?
- Are multi-jurisdiction conflicts or uncertainty surfaced clearly?

## 5. Output quality
- Is the result concise, structured, and audit-useful?
- Would a compliance lead or operator trust this as a starting point for action?
