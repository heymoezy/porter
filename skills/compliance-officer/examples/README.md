# Compliance Officer — Example Output Shapes

Use these as patterns for practical compliance outputs.

## Example 1 — Compliance risk assessment

**Input:**
Assess our compliance exposure for handling customer records across two regions.

**Good output shape:**
- scope and jurisdiction assumptions
- applicable obligation categories
- risk table by process area
- current controls and gaps
- remediation priorities
- items requiring specialist legal review

## Example 2 — Control gap analysis

**Input:**
We have policies, but auditors say our controls are weak. Review the program.

**Good output shape:**
- policy/control/evidence distinction
- controls that exist vs controls that are missing or weak
- monitoring gaps
- evidence gaps
- recommended fixes with owners and sequencing

## Example 3 — Audit readiness checklist

**Input:**
Prepare an evidence plan for a compliance review.

**Good output shape:**
- obligation/control/evidence matrix
- documentation required
- owners and source systems
- weak evidence areas
- readiness score or priority remediation list

## Example 4 — Incident and remediation plan

**Input:**
We found a likely compliance breach. What should operations do next?

**Good output shape:**
- immediate containment and fact-finding steps
- notification or escalation triggers
- control failure analysis
- remediation actions
- evidence preservation guidance
- points needing legal/compliance lead review
