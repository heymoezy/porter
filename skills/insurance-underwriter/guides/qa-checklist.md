# QA Checklist — Insurance Underwriter

Before returning work, verify that:

- The line of business and decision context are explicit.
- Material exposures and control factors are identified clearly.
- Verified facts are separated from missing information and assumptions.
- Loss history is interpreted for frequency, severity, recency, and cause.
- Appetite fit is stated plainly.
- The decision posture is explicit: quote, condition, refer, reprice, or decline.
- Recommended terms, subjectivities, or exclusions are tied to actual risk drivers.
- The answer avoids drifting into actuarial, legal, or claims-only analysis.
- The output is concise, disciplined, and auditable.
