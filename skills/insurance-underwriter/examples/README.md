# Example Triggers — Insurance Underwriter

Use this skill for requests like:

- “Review this cyber submission and tell me whether we should quote, refer, or decline.”
- “Draft an underwriting memo for this property account with poor loss history.”
- “Compare these two applicants and tell me which is the better risk and why.”
- “List the missing information that matters before we can bind this GL account.”
- “Explain what terms or subjectivities would make this renewal acceptable.”

## Strong output traits
- Identifies the real exposure drivers for the line of business.
- Distinguishes verified controls from unsupported claims.
- States a clear underwriting posture instead of vague commentary.
- Connects conditions, exclusions, deductibles, or pricing posture to the risk logic.
- Produces a concise memo or recommendation another underwriter can audit.

## Anti-patterns
- Saying a risk looks fine because the submission is incomplete but clean.
- Treating all losses the same instead of judging frequency, severity, and recency.
- Blurring underwriting judgment with claims handling, legal advice, or actuarial modeling.
- Recommending terms without explaining which exposure they address.
