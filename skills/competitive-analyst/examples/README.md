# Competitive Analyst — Example Output Shapes

Use these as patterns for strong competitive-analysis deliverables.

## Example 1 — Landscape comparison

**Input:**
Compare our B2B invoicing platform against the top 5 alternatives for mid-market finance teams.

**Good output shape:**
- buyer segment and decision criteria
- direct competitors and substitute options
- comparison table across implementation, controls, pricing, and integrations
- where we are stronger, weaker, and undifferentiated
- positioning implications and recommended response

## Example 2 — Battlecard

**Input:**
Create a sales battlecard for deals against Competitor X.

**Good output shape:**
- competitor snapshot and target customer profile
- likely strengths perceived by buyers
- likely weaknesses, tradeoffs, and landmines
- objection-handling guidance
- proof points and questions reps should ask
- red lines: claims sales should not make without evidence

## Example 3 — Pricing move analysis

**Input:**
A rival just launched usage-based pricing. Explain whether we should respond.

**Good output shape:**
- what changed and how confident we are
- likely buyer impact by segment
- implications for win rate, expansion, or churn
- response options: no move, packaging change, messaging change, product change
- recommendation with urgency and rationale

## Example 4 — Win-loss synthesis

**Input:**
Summarize why we lose deals to three recurring competitors.

**Good output shape:**
- loss patterns by segment and use case
- recurring competitor advantages cited by buyers
- gaps that are product, proof, pricing, or sales-execution related
- implications for product and GTM teams
- data gaps that still need validation

## Example 5 — Monitoring brief

**Input:**
Set up a monthly executive summary of important competitor moves in our category.

**Good output shape:**
- major moves grouped by company
- significance rating for each move
- likely effect on buyer perception or market pressure
- watch items for product, marketing, and sales
- actions to take now vs items to monitor only
