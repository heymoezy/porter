# QA Checklist — Competitive Analyst

Use this before finalizing any competitive-analysis output.

## 1. Competitive framing
- Is the focal buyer segment explicit?
- Does the comparison include meaningful substitutes, not just direct rivals?
- Is the decision this analysis supports clearly stated?

## 2. Evidence quality
- Are claims tied to observable signals or clearly labeled inference?
- Are sources recent enough for the pace of the category?
- Are weak or missing data points visible rather than hidden?

## 3. Analytical quality
- Does the analysis compare on real decision drivers, not only feature lists?
- Are strengths, weaknesses, and unknowns balanced fairly?
- Does it distinguish important market moves from cosmetic noise?

## 4. Strategic usefulness
- Are implications translated into product, pricing, messaging, sales, or leadership decisions?
- Is there a recommended response or explicit recommendation to hold steady?
- Would the intended audience know what to do differently after reading it?

## 5. Overall strength
- Does the work help a team compete more intelligently?
- Does it avoid reactive imitation and shallow table-filling?
- Would a senior operator trust this analysis in a real planning or sales context?
