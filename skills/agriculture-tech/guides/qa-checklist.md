# QA Checklist — Agriculture Tech Specialist

Use this before finalizing any agriculture-tech output.

## 1. Problem fit
- Is the core agricultural problem clearly identified?
- Is the recommendation genuinely technology-centered rather than generic farming advice?
- Are crop, geography, and operating context acknowledged where relevant?

## 2. Operational realism
- Does the solution reflect field realities such as connectivity, maintenance, labor, and seasonality?
- Are data collection and calibration burdens considered?
- Does the guidance avoid overcomplicated systems with weak operational payoff?

## 3. Decision workflow quality
- Is there a clear path from data capture to action?
- Are monitoring outputs tied to actual operational decisions?
- Are decision cadence and responsible users identified?

## 4. Technical quality
- Are sensors, remote sensing, dashboards, or traceability systems matched to the use case?
- Are interoperability and integration concerns addressed?
- Are data quality and false-signal risks discussed?

## 5. Outcome clarity
- Are KPIs or success metrics defined?
- Is the implementation phased rather than unrealistically all-at-once?
- Are tradeoffs between cost, complexity, and value made explicit?

## 6. Domain correctness
- Is terminology used correctly?
- Does the recommendation respect climate-smart/resource-efficiency concerns when relevant?
- Does it avoid hype or generic “AI will solve it” framing?

## 7. Output usefulness
- Would a grower, operator, coop, or product team know what to do next?
- Is the result concrete enough to guide a pilot or buying decision?
- Is it concise but complete?
