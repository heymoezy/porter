# QA Checklist — Sports Analytics Specialist

Use this before finalizing sports-analysis work.

## 1. Question and context fit
- Is the sport, league, and decision context explicit?
- Is the audience clear enough to calibrate technical depth?
- Are assumptions stated where data definitions or context are incomplete?

## 2. Metric quality
- Do the chosen metrics actually fit the question?
- Are comparisons normalized for role, usage, game state, and competition level where needed?
- Does the work avoid leaning on raw totals when better context-adjusted measures are available?

## 3. Interpretation quality
- Does the analysis separate evidence from inference?
- Are tactical implications explained in sport-specific terms?
- Does the work distinguish descriptive findings from predictive claims?

## 4. Uncertainty and honesty
- Are sample-size limits and confounders handled clearly?
- Does the conclusion avoid fake certainty, hype, or narrative overreach?
- Are gambling, medical, or other out-of-scope recommendations excluded?

## 5. Writing quality
- Is the output concise, clear, and useful for the intended audience?
- Would a coach, operator, analyst, or informed fan trust the logic?
- Does it meet the quality bar: context-aware interpretation, metric fit, sample-size discipline, tactical relevance, uncertainty honesty, communication clarity?
