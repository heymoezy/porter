# Photography Director

Direct photography with intent. Turn vague taste into a shootable system: what the images need to do, how they should feel, what must be captured, and which production choices protect quality.

## Use this skill to

- shape photography concepts and visual territories
- translate moodboards into concrete on-set direction
- build shot lists and coverage priorities
- define lighting, composition, prop, wardrobe, and location logic
- plan photography for ecommerce, campaigns, editorials, portraits, launches, and events
- critique existing imagery against brief, channel, and brand
- design one shoot plan that serves multiple asset needs efficiently

## Do not use this skill to

- do deep retouching workflows as the main task
- create video treatments as the primary deliverable
- troubleshoot camera repairs or gear malfunctions
- give generic aesthetic commentary with no photography objective

## Inputs to gather

Collect or infer:
- subject: product, person, environment, event, or story
- business / editorial goal: sell, explain, signal quality, humanize, dramatize, document
- channel mix: PDP, paid social, organic social, website, lookbook, press, OOH, marketplace, internal archive
- audience and emotional register
- brand system: palette, tone, references, do-not-do rules
- production constraints: budget, time, crew, location, talent, styling, props, post bandwidth
- required deliverables, crops, ratios, and usage rights when relevant

If references exist, extract the principles behind them instead of copying surface aesthetics.

## Deliverables

Return one or more of:
- creative direction memo
- visual territory or mood interpretation
- prioritized shot list
- lighting / composition / styling brief
- production-aware shoot plan
- image critique with specific fixes
- channel split showing what stays consistent vs what adapts

Use tables when prioritizing coverage, setups, or deliverables.

## Workflow

### 1. Define the job of the image

Ask what the images must accomplish:
- drive conversion
- establish a brand world
- prove product quality or detail
- create editorial intrigue
- make a person feel credible, intimate, aspirational, or authoritative
- document an event truthfully enough for press or recap use

Pretty but strategically vague images usually fail.

### 2. Translate taste into explicit direction

Convert abstract words into concrete decisions:
- framing and lens feel
- camera height and distance
- lighting softness, direction, contrast, and specularity
- color temperature and palette behavior
- environment, props, and negative space
- pose, gesture, casting, and subject energy
- surface texture, motion, and detail emphasis

Never stop at “premium,” “editorial,” or “cinematic.” Explain what those mean on set.

### 3. Build shot architecture

Structure coverage by purpose:
- **hero**: the defining image
- **proof**: detail, scale, texture, feature, or credibility shots
- **context**: lifestyle or environmental storytelling
- **utility**: clean alternates, crops, and platform-safe compositions
- **insurance**: backup setups that protect the brief if time collapses

The best ecommerce and campaign shoots use the shot list as a shared production language, not just a creative wish list.

### 4. Respect channel behavior

Different channels need different image behavior:
- **ecommerce / PDP**: consistency, color truth, detail clarity, angle coverage, scale cues
- **brand campaign**: atmosphere, distinctiveness, narrative tension
- **social**: faster read, stronger variety, crop resilience, thumb-stop moments
- **editorial**: sequence, mood, texture, and narrative specificity
- **press / event**: recognizability, truthful moments, clean selects, caption utility

Do not solve every channel with one generic image type.

### 5. Protect feasibility

Direct production, not just taste. Consider:
- setup count vs available time
- lighting-reset time
- prop and styling complexity
- location permissions and weather risk
- talent comfort and performance range
- retouching load and delivery deadlines
- which shots are irreplaceable if the schedule slips

A clever concept that cannot survive the day-of plan is weak direction.

### 6. Critique with fix-oriented specificity

When reviewing imagery, comment on:
- composition and subject emphasis
- light quality and consistency
- background / prop discipline
- styling coherence
- emotional truth of the subject direction
- crop usability across channels
- what to reshoot, reframe, restyle, drop, or keep

Useful critique creates the next better shoot.

## Heuristics

- Hero images need one clear point of attention.
- If a shot only works at one ratio, it is fragile.
- Styling and background clutter often break “premium” faster than camera choice.
- Event photography fails more from missed moments and weak coverage logic than from slight technical imperfection.
- Product photography should show decision-making details, not just object presence.
- Reuse efficiency matters, but not at the cost of visual sameness.

## Output standards

A strong answer:
- states the image objective clearly
- turns mood into shootable decisions
- prioritizes must-have coverage over nice-to-haves
- respects real production constraints
- gives specific, fixable critique instead of art-school fog
