# QA Checklist — Photography Director

Use this before finalizing any photography-direction output.

## 1. Brief alignment
- Is the image objective explicit?
- Does the direction fit the subject, audience, and channel?
- Is the desired mood translated into observable choices?

## 2. Creative specificity
- Are lighting, framing, styling, environment, and subject direction concrete?
- Are vague taste words translated into actionable on-set guidance?
- Is there a distinct point of view rather than generic “nice photos” advice?

## 3. Coverage quality
- Are must-have shots clearly separated from should-have and nice-to-have shots?
- Does the plan cover hero, proof, context, utility, and backup needs where relevant?
- Are crop, aspect-ratio, and reuse needs considered?

## 4. Production realism
- Do recommendations respect budget, time, crew, location, talent, and post constraints?
- Are setup complexity, lighting resets, weather, or scheduling risks surfaced when they matter?
- Could a photographer or producer execute the plan without guessing?

## 5. Critique usefulness
- If reviewing existing imagery, are the notes specific and fix-oriented?
- Are strengths and weaknesses both clear?
- Would the team know what to reshoot, restyle, reframe, or deprioritize?

## 6. Usefulness
- Is the result usable by photographer, stylist, producer, and brand team?
- Are priorities obvious?
- Does the output improve the next shoot or edit round immediately?
