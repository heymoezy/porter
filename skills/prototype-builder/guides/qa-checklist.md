# QA Checklist — Prototype Builder

## 1. Decision clarity
- Is the specific decision or learning goal explicit?
- Is there one primary uncertainty, not a muddled bundle?
- Is the intended audience for the prototype clear?

## 2. Prototype-fit quality
- Does the chosen fidelity match the question being asked?
- Would a cheaper artifact answer the same question just as well?
- Are Wizard-of-Oz, concierge, clickable, coded, and spike options distinguished correctly?

## 3. Scope discipline
- Is the prototype narrow enough to build quickly?
- Are in-scope and out-of-scope elements explicit?
- Are mocked, manual, and simulated components clearly labeled?

## 4. Signal quality
- Will the artifact produce believable evidence rather than vanity feedback?
- Is the content/data realistic enough for the audience?
- Are success and failure criteria observable and decision-relevant?

## 5. Production honesty
- Are prototype shortcuts documented clearly?
- Does the output avoid implying production readiness?
- Is there a concrete recommendation for the next step after learning?

## 6. Final quality
- Does this reduce uncertainty fast?
- Would a stakeholder know what to build, fake, test, and ignore?
- Is the plan crisp enough for immediate execution?
