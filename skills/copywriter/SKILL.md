# Copywriter

Write copy that moves a specific audience toward a specific action.

This skill is for persuasion work: clarifying promise, tension, proof, differentiation, and CTA so the message does not just read cleanly, but converts better.

## Scope

Use this skill for:
- landing-page and homepage conversion copy
- headlines, subheads, hooks, and CTA language
- ad copy and campaign messaging
- product marketing and launch copy
- email sequences and nurture flows
- sales-enablement messaging and objection handling
- offer positioning and value-proposition sharpening
- rewrite of weak copy with stronger persuasion logic

## Use this skill when

Use this skill when the task needs:
- stronger persuasion, not just cleaner prose
- message-to-market alignment
- clear offer framing and differentiation
- concise copy that earns attention quickly
- variants for testing angles, hooks, or CTAs

## Do not use this skill when

Do not use this skill for:
- long-form editorial drafting where content-writer or blog-writer fits better
- full brand-system creation where brand-designer is primary
- channel planning, segmentation strategy, or campaign orchestration by itself
- factual/legal review of claims in regulated contexts without the right specialist

## Inputs to gather

Before writing, identify:
- audience and stage of awareness
- offer, product, or action being promoted
- primary pain, desire, or job-to-be-done
- proof points, objections, and differentiators
- channel and placement constraints
- brand voice, risk tolerance, and forbidden claims
- success metric: click, signup, reply, demo, purchase, retention, etc.

If critical proof is missing, write with disciplined placeholders instead of fabricating credibility.

## Output expectations

Return outputs such as:
- polished conversion copy draft
- headline and angle options
- messaging hierarchy: hook, promise, proof, CTA
- control version plus 2–5 focused variants
- objection-handling bullets or FAQ copy
- short rationale explaining why a framing should work

Prioritize strongest options, not option spam.

## Working method

### 1. Define the conversion job

Clarify:
- who is this for?
- what action should happen next?
- what friction is stopping that action now?
- what belief must change for the action to feel obvious?

Persuasion starts with the decision, not the sentence.

### 2. Isolate the core promise

State the sharpest credible promise:
- desired outcome
- timeframe or speed if supportable
- pain removed or gain created
- who it is especially for

A vague promise produces forgettable copy.

### 3. Build the persuasion spine

Organize copy around a usable structure. Common patterns:
- problem → agitation → solution
- attention → interest → desire → action
- before → after → bridge
- job-to-be-done → obstacle → mechanism → proof → CTA

Choose the pattern that matches the reader’s awareness level and the channel.

### 4. Handle objections before polishing style

Look for resistance such as:
- “This is not for me.”
- “I do not believe the claim.”
- “It sounds hard or risky.”
- “I can wait.”
- “A competitor sounds similar.”

Address objections with specificity, proof, and friction reduction.

### 5. Make proof carry the weight

Use proof like:
- quantified outcomes
- customer examples
- process clarity
- credible constraints and caveats
- comparisons, if supportable

Good copy does not shout when evidence can do the work.

### 6. Tighten for speed and force

Revise for:
- stronger verbs
- fewer abstractions
- shorter openings
- clearer visual hierarchy
- CTA language that names the next step plainly

Every line should either earn attention, build belief, or drive action.

### 7. Produce testable variants

When variants matter, vary one thing at a time:
- angle
- audience emphasis
- proof style
- urgency framing
- CTA wording

Do not generate random alternatives with no testing logic.

## Adjacent skill boundaries

- **content-writer**: clearer general drafting; this skill owns higher-stakes persuasion and conversion pressure
- **email-writer**: deeper email-format nuance when the whole task is email-first
- **seo-specialist**: owns search-intent strategy and ranking considerations
- **creative-strategist**: owns broader campaign concept and positioning direction beyond the copy itself
- **brand-designer**: owns visual/verbal identity systems, not just response-driving messages

## Quality bar

A strong result should:
- make the audience and action unmistakably clear
- sharpen value, differentiation, and proof
- remove friction and answer likely objections
- fit the channel’s length and rhythm
- feel usable in a real conversion test, not like generic marketing filler
