# QA Checklist — Operations Manager

Use this before finalizing any operations-focused output.

## 1. Scope and objective
- Is the recurring operating problem clearly defined?
- Is the service promise or objective explicit?
- Does the answer distinguish ongoing operations from one-off project work?

## 2. Workflow completeness
- Are intake, triage, routing, execution, QA, escalation, and closure covered where relevant?
- Are waiting states, dependencies, and handoffs visible?
- Are exception and breach paths defined, not just the normal path?

## 3. Ownership quality
- Does every critical step have an accountable owner?
- Are decision rights and escalation thresholds clear?
- Would a new operator know who acts when something breaks?

## 4. Capacity and control realism
- Does the design account for staffing, shifts, skill tiers, or overflow?
- Are tooling or manual-work constraints acknowledged?
- Does the recommendation avoid hiding chronic under-capacity behind process language?

## 5. Metrics and governance
- Are KPIs few, actionable, and tied to decisions?
- Is there a review cadence with named owners?
- Do thresholds trigger a concrete response?

## 6. Practical usefulness
- Is the process teachable and runnable next week?
- Are SOP steps short, observable, and auditable?
- Are rollout steps, risks, and next actions clear?
