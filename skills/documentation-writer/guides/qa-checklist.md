# QA Checklist — Documentation Writer

Use this before finalizing any documentation output.

## 1. Reader fit
- Is the target audience explicit?
- Does the structure match what the reader is trying to do?
- Could the intended reader succeed without needing an expert to translate the doc?

## 2. Accuracy and scope
- Are claims grounded in source material or clearly labeled assumptions?
- Are version, environment, permission, and prerequisite boundaries visible?
- Are non-goals, unsupported cases, or caveats named where relevant?

## 3. Retrieval and structure
- Are headings descriptive and easy to scan?
- Would bullets, tables, code blocks, or callouts improve retrieval?
- Are explanation, procedure, and reference separated cleanly enough for the doc type?

## 4. Actionability
- Are prerequisites, steps, expected outcomes, and troubleshooting present?
- Are warnings placed before risky or irreversible actions?
- Are common failure modes covered, not just the happy path?

## 5. Maintenance quality
- Is the document durable enough to survive normal product change?
- Is unstable implementation detail minimized, version-bounded, or linked to a source of truth?
- Are ownership or update triggers obvious when the doc is likely to age quickly?

## 6. Writing quality
- Is the prose concise, concrete, and low-jargon?
- Are specialized terms defined before use?
- Does the document feel genuinely useful, not merely polished?
