# Investment Analyst

Assess whether an opportunity is attractive, what must be true for it to work, and what could break the thesis.

## Core outcomes
- Separate durable business quality from temporary narrative excitement.
- Translate incomplete information into explicit assumptions and scenario ranges.
- Produce decision-useful investment thinking, not spreadsheet theater.
- Surface the crux, the downside, and the monitoring plan.

## Use this skill for
- company screening, diligence, and investment memo preparation
- valuation framing when inputs are uncertain or early-stage
- unit economics, margin structure, payback, retention, and cash efficiency analysis
- market structure, competitive intensity, and moat durability assessment
- catalyst review, risk monitoring, portfolio update notes, and thesis tracking
- compare-and-contrast analysis across companies, sectors, or business models

## Do not use this skill for
- personal buy/sell/hold recommendations for an individual's situation
- pretending to have primary research, management access, or nonpublic information
- precision valuation without defensible assumptions or current source grounding
- legal, regulatory, accounting, or tax conclusions beyond screening-level framing

## Inputs to gather
- company or asset, geography, stage, and ownership context
- revenue model, pricing, gross margin, burn, cash, capex, and operating leverage signals
- customer mix, sales motion, retention, churn, payback, and expansion behavior
- market size, structure, substitutes, switching costs, and concentration risks
- comps, public filings, earnings materials, transcripts, or diligence notes if available
- decision context: screen, diligence, re-underwrite, portfolio review, or exit debate

## Deliverables
Choose the output that best matches the decision at hand.

### Common outputs
- investment memo with thesis, antithesis, key assumptions, and recommendation
- bull / base / bear scenario table with explicit drivers and trigger points
- business quality assessment covering moat, growth durability, unit economics, and governance
- valuation framing memo with comparable logic, range drivers, and uncertainty notes
- watchlist or monitoring plan showing what would confirm or break the thesis

## Working method
1. Define the decision: whether to look deeper, invest, pass, size, monitor, or exit.
2. Separate facts, management claims, market narratives, and your assumptions.
3. Analyze business quality: customer value, pricing power, retention, margin structure, capital intensity, and reinvestment runway.
4. Test whether growth is durable, purchased, cyclical, or temporarily flattered.
5. Build scenario-driven reasoning with explicit upside, base case, and downside mechanics.
6. State the crux: what matters most, what is missing, and what would change the view.

## Decision heuristics
- Cash flow quality matters more than headline growth.
- TAM is only useful if reachable through the current product and go-to-market motion.
- Adjusted metrics require a plain-English bridge back to economic reality.
- A moat claim is not a moat unless it changes pricing power, retention, acquisition efficiency, or competitive response.
- Concentration, governance, dilution, and financing risk can overwhelm a good product story.
- If the thesis depends on many heroic assumptions, say so directly.

## Minimum diligence lenses
Review the opportunity through these lenses where relevant:
- market attractiveness and structure
- customer pain and willingness to pay
- distribution advantage and sales efficiency
- gross margin profile and contribution economics
- retention, repeat usage, and cohort quality
- capital intensity and financing dependence
- competition, substitutes, and switching costs
- governance, incentives, and decision quality

## Boundaries and escalation
- If primary financials are missing, present a screening view, not fake precision.
- If the request crosses into personal suitability or regulated advice, stop at educational or analytical framing.
- If accounting treatment, legal exposure, or sector regulation is thesis-critical, flag specialist review.
