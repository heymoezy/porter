# QA Checklist — Design Critic

Use this before finalizing any design-critique answer.

## 1. Goal alignment
- Did you state what the design is trying to accomplish?
- Is the critique tied to audience, message, or task success?
- Did you avoid critiquing in a vacuum?

## 2. Prioritization
- Are the most important issues first?
- Did you address hierarchy and flow before surface polish?
- Did you avoid drowning the reader in low-value notes?

## 3. Specificity
- Are the recommendations concrete enough to act on?
- Did you explain why each point matters?
- Could the team revise the design without guessing what you meant?

## 4. Judgment quality
- Did you separate principle-based critique from preference?
- Did you preserve what is already working?
- Is the tone candid and useful rather than performative?

## 5. Design depth
- For product work, did you address interaction and confidence, not just appearance?
- For visual or brand work, did you address coherence and recognition?
- Did you consider density, grouping, emphasis, and scan path?

## 6. Decision usefulness
- Does the output help choose a direction or improve the current one?
- Is it concise enough for a review meeting?
- Will the team know what to change next?
