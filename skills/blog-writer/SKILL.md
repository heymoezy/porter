# Blog Writer

Write posts people actually finish.

This skill is for producing blog content that has a job to do: attract, educate, persuade, or retain attention. Strong blog writing balances clarity, voice, structure, factual support, and distribution intent instead of stuffing keywords into generic prose.

## Scope

Use this skill for:
- SEO blog posts and pillar content
- thought-leadership articles
- educational explainers and how-to posts
- product or feature announcement posts in blog form
- customer story or case-study-style blog adaptations
- editorial refreshes of weak or outdated posts
- blog outlines, headline options, and angle development

## Do not use this skill for

Do not use this skill for:
- ad copy, landing-page copy, or email campaigns as the main deliverable
- deeply technical documentation or API reference material
- ghostwritten executive speeches or scripts
- purely journalistic reporting that requires original field reporting you cannot verify
- SEO content mills optimized for keyword stuffing over reader value

## Inputs to gather

Before drafting, identify:
- target audience and what they already know
- the post's business goal and desired reader action
- primary topic, angle, and distribution channel
- source material, evidence, product facts, and claims that must be verified
- target length, structure, tone, and style constraints
- primary and secondary keywords if SEO matters
- internal links, calls to action, and conversion path
- whether the piece should lead with insight, story, utility, or news

If the brief has no angle, create one. A blog post without a point of view becomes wallpaper.

## Output expectations

Return outputs such as:
- full blog draft
- outline with headline and section recommendations
- editorial rewrite of an existing post
- SEO refresh plan for a weak article
- content brief with audience, angle, and keyword strategy
- versioned headline, intro, and CTA options

## Working method

### 1. Start with the reader problem and the post's job

Decide what the post needs to accomplish:
- answer a question
- reframe a problem
- teach a process
- build trust in a point of view
- move readers toward a product or next action

Good posts are useful before they are optimized.

### 2. Choose a clear angle

Many topics are too broad to be interesting.
Sharpen the piece with an angle such as:
- what changed
- what most people get wrong
- a framework or decision lens
- a contrarian perspective supported by evidence
- a step-by-step path tied to a real outcome

If you cannot summarize the thesis in one sentence, the draft will sprawl.

### 3. Build structure before writing paragraphs

Create an outline with:
- working headline
- strong opening hook
- 3 to 6 major sections
- examples, evidence, or stories supporting each section
- conclusion and CTA

Structure is where most weak blog content fails.

### 4. Write for momentum and clarity

Use:
- short paragraphs
- concrete examples
- subheads that carry meaning
- active voice
- transitions that move the reader forward

Cut throat-clearing. The reader should get value fast.

### 5. Handle SEO as support, not the main event

If SEO applies:
- place primary keywords naturally in high-signal locations
- answer likely search intent clearly
- cover related concepts the reader actually cares about
- add internal linking opportunities
- avoid robotic repetition or filler sections added only for ranking

### 6. Tighten claims and finish with purpose

Before finalizing:
- verify facts and product statements
- remove unsupported hype
- sharpen the headline and intro
- make the CTA fit the reader's stage of awareness
- ensure the post feels like it was written by someone with a view, not by template

## Heuristics

Prefer:
- a strong thesis over a broad topic sweep
- specific examples over vague advice
- practical utility plus voice
- readable structure with meaningful subheads
- honest, evidence-backed claims

Avoid:
- keyword-stuffed paragraphs
- intros that take too long to say anything
- empty thought leadership with no concrete takeaway
- generic conclusions that repeat the post without moving the reader
- tone that sounds interchangeable with every other company blog

## Review lenses

When evaluating blog work, check:
- Is the audience clear?
- Does the piece have a distinct angle or thesis?
- Does the structure pull the reader through the post?
- Are claims accurate and supported?
- Is SEO present without degrading readability?
- Does the CTA match the post's purpose?

## Adjacent skill boundaries

- **content-writer**: broader content production outside blog-specific editorial strategy
- **copywriter**: shorter conversion-focused copy
- **technical-writer**: documentation and instructional material with higher precision demands
- **case-study-writer**: deeper customer narrative with proof and outcome framing
- **seo-specialist**: search strategy, keyword research, and optimization beyond the article itself

## Quality bar

A strong result should:
- earn attention with a clear angle
- deliver useful information quickly
- sound intentional, not generic
- support business goals without feeling like a sales brochure
- leave the reader knowing what to think, do, or read next
