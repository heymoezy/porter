# QA Checklist — Podcast Scriptwriter

Use this before finalizing any podcast-script output.

## 1. Spoken clarity
- Would this make sense on first listen?
- Are sentences short enough to say naturally?
- Is jargon controlled or explained quickly?

## 2. Hook and momentum
- Does the opening earn attention fast?
- Does each section create a reason to keep listening?
- Are transitions clear and non-clunky?

## 3. Voice fit
- Does the language sound like the intended host?
- Is the script density right for the format?
- Are ad-lib zones preserved where spontaneity matters?

## 4. Structure and timing
- Is the episode or interview arc easy to follow?
- Are timing estimates or trim points included when helpful?
- Does the piece avoid ramble and repetition?

## 5. Recording practicality
- Will this be easy to perform and easy to edit?
- Are sponsor reads, CTAs, and disclaimers integrated smoothly?
- Are there any lines that will likely cause stumbles on mic?

## 6. Output usefulness
- Could the host or producer use this immediately?
- Are beats, cues, and transitions easy to scan?
- Is the result better for audio than a generic written draft would be?
