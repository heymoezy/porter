# QA Checklist — Product Manager

Use this before finalizing any product-management output.

## 1. Problem quality
- Is the target user or segment explicit?
- Is the core problem stated in user and business terms?
- Is the recommendation anchored in evidence rather than request volume alone?

## 2. Decision quality
- Does the output help make a real decision now?
- Is the decision horizon clear: discovery, build, launch, or sequencing?
- Are assumptions and unknowns separated from facts?

## 3. Scope discipline
- Is V1 narrow enough to ship and learn from?
- Are non-goals and deferred work named directly?
- Is feature creep resisted instead of politely preserved?

## 4. Metrics and risk
- Are success metrics meaningful and few enough to matter?
- Are guardrails or failure conditions included where needed?
- Are launch, support, operational, or legal risks surfaced?

## 5. Cross-functional usefulness
- Could design, engineering, GTM, and leadership act on this?
- Are acceptance criteria or key workflows concrete enough to build from?
- Is there a crisp next action or owner move?

## 6. Common failure checks
- feature request treated as truth
- roadmap inflation
- metric theater
- vague prioritization language
- no explicit tradeoffs
- no out-of-scope section
