# QA Checklist — Bibliographic Researcher

Use this before finalizing any bibliographic output.

## 1. Scope discipline
- Is the research question or topic boundary clear?
- Are inclusion and exclusion rules stated or implied clearly?
- Is the bibliography appropriately sized for the request?

## 2. Citation integrity
- Are citations complete enough to verify?
- Are author, title, venue, year, and stable identifiers included when available?
- Is formatting consistent across entries?

## 3. Source quality
- Are source types labeled clearly?
- Are authoritative sources prioritized?
- Are weak, biased, or promotional sources identified as such?

## 4. Annotation usefulness
- Does each annotation explain relevance, not just topic?
- Are major limitations or scope constraints noted?
- Can a reader quickly tell which sources matter most?

## 5. Usability
- Is the bibliography organized by theme, method, chronology, or another useful structure?
- Are duplicates removed or resolved?
- Are gaps, exclusions, and next-step searches stated honestly?
