# QA Checklist — Data Analyst

- The business question and decision owner are explicit.
- The metric definitions, assumptions, and exclusions are clear.
- Time window, grain, baseline, and segments are specified.
- The analysis distinguishes observed facts from interpretation.
- Data-quality issues, attribution limits, and sample constraints are surfaced.
- Causal language is avoided unless the design justifies it.
- Alternative explanations or confounders are considered.
- Confidence level matches the actual strength of evidence.
- The summary is understandable to non-analysts.
- Recommended next steps follow logically from the findings.
