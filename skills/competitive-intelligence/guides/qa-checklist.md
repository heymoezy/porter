# QA Checklist — Competitive Intelligence

Use this before finalizing any competitive-intelligence output.

## 1. Purpose and framing
- Is the intelligence purpose explicit?
- Are the right competitors and substitutes being monitored?
- Is the audience for the intelligence clear?

## 2. Signal quality
- Are sources credible and appropriately weighted?
- Are observed facts separated from interpretation?
- Are low-signal or cosmetic updates filtered appropriately?

## 3. Operational quality
- Is there a clear cadence, ownership, or update rhythm?
- Are alert thresholds or significance levels defined?
- Could this actually run as an intelligence practice, not just a one-time memo?

## 4. Strategic usefulness
- Does the output explain why signals matter?
- Are implications translated into audience-specific action or watch items?
- Does it help the team see threats or opportunities earlier?

## 5. Ethics and defensibility
- Are collection methods ethical and defensible?
- Are weak inferences labeled as weak?
- Would a senior operator trust this output in a real planning context?
