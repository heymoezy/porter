# QA Checklist — Directive Librarian

Use this before finalizing.

## 1. Provenance
- Did you identify where the guidance came from?
- Is the evidence or authority explicit?
- Did you avoid overstating weak sources as settled policy?

## 2. Status discipline
- Is the item labeled active, proposed, disputed, deprecated, or informational?
- Does the chosen status match the evidence and approval state?
- Are unresolved conflicts still visible?

## 3. Scope clarity
- Did you specify actors, systems, triggers, and exceptions?
- Is it clear whether the directive is global, local, temporary, or emergency-only?
- Did you avoid vague universal wording?

## 4. Conflict handling
- Did you check for duplicates, overlap, or supersession?
- Are contradictions surfaced explicitly?
- Is there a clear merge, deprecate, or escalate recommendation?

## 5. Retrieval usefulness
- Is the directive concise and operationally worded?
- Could a future agent retrieve and apply it correctly?
- Are related directives or dependencies noted where useful?

## 6. Decision trail
- Did you leave a clear next action, reviewer, or approver?
- Is the rationale visible enough to trust the classification later?
- Would this improve the reliability of the directive corpus over time?
