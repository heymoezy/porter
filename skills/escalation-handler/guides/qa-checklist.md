# QA Checklist — Escalation Handler

Use this before finalizing any escalation output.

## 1. Control and ownership
- Is severity or impact level stated?
- Is there one coordinating owner plus clear supporting owners?
- Are actions and deadlines assigned explicitly?

## 2. Communication quality
- Does the message acknowledge the actual impact?
- Are knowns, unknowns, and active work separated clearly?
- Is the next update time explicit and easy to find?

## 3. Trust management
- Does the response avoid speculation and false reassurance?
- Is the tone calm, accountable, and proportional to the issue?
- Are missed commitments or repeat-failure dynamics addressed where relevant?

## 4. Operational usefulness
- Could internal teams execute from this plan immediately?
- Are dependencies, blockers, and escalation paths visible?
- Does the output make coordination easier rather than noisier?

## 5. Resolution and learning
- Is there a clear path to verify actual resolution?
- Are trust-repair or prevention actions named?
- Does the output close the loop rather than stopping at apology?

## 6. Writing quality
- Is the deliverable concise and easy to scan under stress?
- Are responsibility and timing easy to spot?
- Does the result make the situation feel more controlled?
