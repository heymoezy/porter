# QA Checklist — ux-researcher

- The artifact, target users, and decision under test are explicit.
- The work is clearly about a concrete experience, not broad discovery research.
- Tasks reflect real user goals rather than button-by-button instructions.
- Success, failure, confusion, and recovery criteria are defined.
- Findings separate severity, frequency, impact, and likely cause.
- Recommendations connect directly to observed usability failures.
- Risky states such as errors, empty states, permissions, or destructive actions are covered when relevant.
- Evidence is prioritized; not every observation is treated as equally important.
- Remaining risk and confidence limits are explicit.
- The result is concise, structured, and launch-useful.
