# QA Checklist — Regulatory Analyst

## 1. Source discipline
- Are the jurisdiction, regulator, and source type explicit?
- Were primary sources prioritized where available?
- Are citations, section references, or clearly attributable source references included?

## 2. Applicability quality
- Does the analysis explain who the rule applies to and why?
- Are entity type, product scope, thresholds, and exemptions handled carefully?
- Are multiple jurisdictions separated rather than blended?

## 3. Obligation extraction
- Are binding duties, prohibitions, deadlines, and evidence requirements captured?
- Are defined terms and trigger conditions preserved accurately?
- Are guidance materials distinguished from mandatory requirements?

## 4. Operational translation
- Are obligations mapped to controls, teams, processes, or evidence?
- Could an operator understand what must change?
- Are implementation priorities and deadlines clear?

## 5. Uncertainty handling
- Are assumptions stated explicitly?
- Are ambiguous issues or unresolved interpretation questions surfaced?
- Is the output disciplined about when counsel is needed?

## 6. Final bar
- Is the answer source-faithful rather than generic?
- Would compliance leadership find it actionable?
- Would legal counsel see it as careful and non-sloppy?
