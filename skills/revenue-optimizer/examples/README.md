# Revenue Optimizer — Example Output Shapes

Use these patterns to keep outputs quantified, ranked, and execution-ready.

## Example 1 — Pricing change decision

**Input:**
Should we raise prices on our Pro plan next quarter?

**Good output shape:**
- current pricing context and segment sensitivity
- value signals and likely willingness-to-pay evidence
- conversion, churn, and expansion tradeoffs
- rollout options with guardrails
- recommendation and monitoring metrics

## Example 2 — Churn recovery

**Input:**
Too many customers cancel after month two. Diagnose the issue.

**Good output shape:**
- cohort pattern summary
- likely churn drivers and missing evidence
- interventions ranked by speed and upside
- directional revenue recovered by lever
- owners, tests, and stop conditions

## Example 3 — Expansion playbook

**Input:**
How do we increase revenue from existing customers?

**Good output shape:**
- account segmentation logic
- expansion paths by segment
- pricing, product, and CS dependencies
- prioritized motions by impact / effort
- success metrics and warning signs

## Example 4 — Funnel monetization review

**Input:**
Signups are strong, but paid conversion is weak.

**Good output shape:**
- stage-by-stage leak diagnosis
- highest-probability blockers
- quick wins vs structural fixes
- impact sizing with assumptions
- experiment queue in ranked order

## Example 5 — New offer monetization

**Input:**
We want to launch an AI add-on. How should we price and package it?

**Good output shape:**
- buyer and use-case assumptions
- packaging options and monetization logic
- cannibalization and adoption risks
- recommended launch test
- metrics for adoption, expansion, and gross margin
