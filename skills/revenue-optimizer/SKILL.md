# Revenue Optimizer

Grow revenue with discipline, not wishful thinking.

## Mission

Use this skill to find where revenue is actually won or lost, estimate which levers matter most, and turn commercial diagnosis into a ranked action plan. The job is not to brainstorm endlessly. The job is to identify the few bets worth making.

## Best-fit work

Use this skill for:
- pricing and packaging reviews
- monetization design for new offers, add-ons, tiers, or usage models
- funnel diagnosis from lead to closed-won to retained revenue
- churn, contraction, and renewal leakage analysis
- expansion, upsell, cross-sell, and account-growth planning
- sales-efficiency and discount-discipline reviews
- revenue experiment prioritization with measurable success criteria

## Do not use this skill for

Do not use this skill for:
- unsupported top-line forecasts stated as certainty
- accounting, tax, or revenue-recognition decisions
- generic growth brainstorming disconnected from numbers
- optimizing traffic or engagement metrics that do not change monetization
- product or brand strategy where commercial mechanics are not the core question

## Inputs to gather

Lock down as many of these as possible:
- revenue model: subscription, usage-based, transactional, services, marketplace, hybrid
- pricing, packaging, contract terms, discounts, and renewal structure
- funnel performance by stage, segment, channel, and cohort
- retention, churn, contraction, expansion, and net revenue retention patterns
- customer segments, willingness-to-pay clues, and usage/value realization signals
- gross margin, cost-to-serve, sales capacity, and implementation constraints
- risk tolerance, timeline, and operational ability to ship changes

If core denominators are missing, say so. Weak baselines create fake strategy.

## Working method

### 1. Map the revenue engine

Separate acquisition, activation, conversion, retention, expansion, and price realization. Most teams talk about “revenue” as one problem when the leak is localized.

### 2. Find the binding constraint

Identify the commercial bottleneck with the highest leverage. Examples: weak value communication, discount leakage, low trial conversion, poor onboarding, bad-fit acquisition, underpriced usage, or flat post-sale expansion.

### 3. Size the opportunity directionally

Estimate impact with simple transparent math. Use ranges when inputs are uncertain. A rough model with visible assumptions beats confident fiction.

### 4. Protect revenue quality

Consider churn, support load, implementation burden, gross margin, trust, and brand effects. More bookings are not automatically better revenue.

### 5. Prioritize by impact × confidence × speed

Recommend what the team can actually execute. A smaller, faster lever with clean instrumentation may beat a theoretically huge but organizationally unrealistic change.

### 6. Convert recommendations into tests

For each priority lever, define owner, segment, rollout path, success metric, guardrails, and stop conditions.

## Heuristics

Prefer:
- segment-specific recommendations over one-size-fits-all pricing changes
- retention and expansion fixes before extra acquisition spend when revenue leaks downstream
- pricing clarity and buyer fit over packaging gimmicks
- explicit assumptions, ranges, and sensitivity notes
- a ranked short list of revenue bets

Avoid:
- treating all ARR as equally healthy
- recommending price increases without value proof and churn-risk analysis
- confusing bookings, billings, recognized revenue, cash, and margin
- copying competitor pricing without understanding their motion or economics
- presenting an unranked experiment backlog as strategy

## Output expectations

Strong outputs usually include:
- revenue-engine diagnosis
- top levers ranked by expected impact, confidence, and effort
- directional sizing and key assumptions
- execution dependencies and risks
- test design, owners, and guardrails
- recommendation on what to do first and what to ignore for now

## Adjacent skill boundaries

- **pricing-strategist**: deeper pricing architecture and packaging design as the primary problem
- **financial-analyst**: full financial modeling, forecasting, and scenario analysis
- **customer-success-manager**: post-sale adoption and renewal operations as the main focus
- **go-to-market-planner**: broader market, channel, and launch strategy beyond revenue mechanics

## Quality bar

A strong result should:
- identify the real commercial bottleneck
- quantify upside without pretending certainty
- protect retention, margin, and trust
- recommend actions the team can execute soon
- turn strategy into testable operational next steps
