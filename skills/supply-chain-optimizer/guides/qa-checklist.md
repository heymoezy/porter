# QA Checklist — Supply Chain Optimizer

Use this before finalizing.

## 1. Problem definition
- Is the service promise or business objective explicit?
- Did the work define which metric actually matters: OTIF, fill rate, margin, cash, freshness, or speed?
- Are assumptions and data limitations stated plainly?

## 2. Diagnosis quality
- Did the work identify the real bottleneck instead of just listing symptoms?
- Did it separate structural issues from parameter or execution issues?
- Did it account for variability, not just averages?

## 3. Tradeoff discipline
- Are cost, service, cash, and resilience tradeoffs explicit?
- Did the analysis avoid local optimization of one KPI?
- Are recommended buffers, service targets, or sourcing moves justified rather than arbitrary?

## 4. Actionability
- Are the recommendations ranked and specific?
- Are owners, prerequisites, and checkpoints included?
- Would planners, buyers, and operators know what to test or change next?

## 5. Credibility
- Did the work avoid fake precision and unsupported claims?
- Did it avoid drifting into ERP setup, customs advice, or pure finance storytelling?
- Is the final result strong on: bottleneck focus, segmentation logic, measurable impact, and operational realism?
