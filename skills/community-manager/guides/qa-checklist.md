# QA Checklist — Community Manager

Use this before finalizing any community-management output.

## 1. Community diagnosis
- Is the community’s core purpose stated clearly?
- Are member segments and desired behaviors identified?
- Does the analysis separate engagement issues from trust or moderation issues?

## 2. Strategy and operations
- Are recommendations concrete enough to run, staff, and measure?
- Do rituals, campaigns, or programs map to specific member behaviors?
- Is operational load realistic for the team size?

## 3. Moderation quality
- Are rules specific, enforceable, and easy to interpret?
- Are escalation paths defined for sensitive or high-risk cases?
- Do templates preserve dignity while maintaining clear boundaries?

## 4. Metrics and feedback loops
- Are health metrics meaningful beyond raw member counts?
- Are response quality, retention, and unanswered demand considered?
- Is there a clear cadence for reviewing community health and adjusting playbooks?

## 5. Overall strength
- Would this work make the community feel more active, safer, and more useful?
- Does it avoid shallow engagement theater?
- Would an experienced operator trust this as a practical plan rather than a generic brainstorm?
