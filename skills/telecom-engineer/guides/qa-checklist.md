# QA Checklist — Telecom Engineer

- The relevant telecom path is mapped clearly.
- Signaling, media, numbering, routing, carrier, and application layers are separated where relevant.
- Root-cause hypotheses are ranked and tied to observable evidence.
- Protocol details such as SDP, RTP, codecs, sender type, or carrier error codes are used accurately.
- The output distinguishes requester-controlled fixes from carrier- or destination-network-controlled issues.
- Regional rules, numbering format, compliance, fraud, or throughput constraints are covered when relevant.
- Recommended changes are observable, reversible, and operationally safe.
- Validation steps include traces, metrics, or controlled tests.
- Rollback or escalation guidance is included when the change could create service impact.
- The writing is specific enough for an operator or telecom engineer to act on.
