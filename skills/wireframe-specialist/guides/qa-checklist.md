# QA Checklist — wireframe-specialist

- The user goal, success condition, and key constraints are explicit.
- The output chooses the right artifact type: screen list, wireflow, comparison, or state map.
- Each major screen or step has a clear purpose and primary action.
- Hierarchy and grouping are concrete rather than implied.
- Branches, dependencies, and recovery paths are visible where relevant.
- Empty, loading, success, error, permission, and edge states are covered when they matter.
- Fidelity stays low or mid-fi unless a stronger reason is stated.
- Annotations clarify risky behavior, requirements, or dependencies rather than narrating the obvious.
- Assumptions and unresolved product questions are called out directly.
- The result is concise, structured, and immediately useful to product, design, and engineering.
