# QA Checklist — Technical Writer

- The intended reader and their task are clear.
- The document shape fits the job: explainer, procedure, reference, release note, or migration guide.
- All prerequisites, permissions, versions, and environmental assumptions are stated.
- Steps are complete, ordered, and free of hidden dependencies.
- Terms, command names, field names, and behavioral descriptions are technically consistent.
- Examples are realistic, correctly formatted, and placed near the relevant concept or step.
- Caveats, known issues, and unsupported paths are visible where they matter.
- Verification steps let the reader confirm success.
- Rollback, mitigation, or escalation guidance is included when failure has meaningful consequences.
- The writing is concise, scannable, and free of filler or marketing tone.
