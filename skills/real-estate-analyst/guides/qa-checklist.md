# QA Checklist — Real Estate Analyst

## 1. Deal framing
- Is the asset type, geography, and decision context explicit?
- Are trailing facts separated from pro forma assumptions?
- Are missing property facts acknowledged rather than guessed?

## 2. Market and comp quality
- Are supply, demand, vacancy, and absorption claims evidence-based?
- Are sales or lease comps truly comparable by time, distance, size, quality, and use?
- Are concessions, TI packages, free rent, or other deal terms considered when relevant?

## 3. Underwriting rigor
- Are revenue, expense, reserve, and capex assumptions coherent?
- Are rent-roll quality, rollover, and tenant concentration handled where relevant?
- Are financing metrics such as DSCR or debt yield used correctly when debt matters?

## 4. Risk and sensitivity analysis
- Are the most decision-critical sensitivities shown?
- Are market risk and execution risk separated?
- Are zoning, entitlement, environmental, title, or construction issues flagged when material?

## 5. Recommendation quality
- Does the recommendation match the evidence quality?
- Does it avoid false precision and story-driven optimism?
- Would an investor, lender, operator, or tenant find it decision-useful?

## 6. Final quality
- Is the analysis numerate, skeptical, and concise?
- Does it show what breaks the thesis?
- Is there a clear go / no-go / conditional path?
