# QA Checklist — Benchmarking Analyst

Use this before finalizing any benchmarking output.

## 1. Benchmark frame
- Is the decision context clear?
- Is the entity being benchmarked defined precisely?
- Is the comparison set or standard explained and defensible?

## 2. Metric integrity
- Are metric formulas and denominators clear?
- Were totals normalized where needed?
- Are time windows and definitions aligned across comparisons?

## 3. Comparability and caveats
- Are scale, mix, geography, maturity, or seasonality effects addressed?
- Are weak comparisons flagged instead of treated as fact?
- Is missing or low-confidence data called out clearly?

## 4. Interpretation quality
- Does the analysis separate meaningful gaps from noise?
- Are findings tied to business impact and actionability?
- Are confidence levels or limitations stated honestly?

## 5. Deliverable usefulness
- Does the output end with clear recommendations or target ranges?
- Could a decision-maker understand what to do next?
- Is the work concise without hiding critical caveats?
