# QA Checklist — Print Designer

Use this before finalizing any print-design output.

## 1. Job framing
- Is the piece type and final physical format clear?
- Are viewing distance, use context, and print method stated when relevant?
- Are assumptions separated from confirmed printer specs?

## 2. Production geometry
- Are bleed, trim, and safe-zone requirements addressed?
- Are folds, spines, gutters, wraps, or mounting zones considered where relevant?
- Is content placement checked against likely trim and finishing tolerance?

## 3. Asset and color realism
- Are image-resolution comments tied to final placed size?
- Are color-space assumptions appropriate for the print process?
- Are rich black, spot color, tint, overprint, or transparency risks flagged when relevant?

## 4. Readability and craft
- Will the hierarchy work at real viewing distance?
- Is type likely to remain legible on paper and stock, not just on screen?
- Are thin rules, reverse type, small labels, and barcode/QR usage checked realistically?

## 5. Handoff quality
- Could a designer or production manager act on the output immediately?
- Are export recommendations and proofing needs explicit?
- Are unresolved questions clearly routed to the printer or fabricator?

## 6. Common misses
- missing bleed
- content too close to trim or fold
- RGB assumptions left unresolved
- low-resolution imagery at final size
- fragile fine detail likely to fail in production
