# QA Checklist — tmux

- The answer justifies using tmux instead of assuming it by default.
- Session, window, and pane structure is clear, readable, and matched to the workflow.
- Commands or key sequences are exact enough to execute.
- Attach, detach, reattach, and recovery steps are included for durable workflows.
- Output capture, copy mode, or scrollback guidance is present when debugging or evidence matters.
- Advice for live-pane automation includes explicit target/context assumptions and safety cautions.
- The workflow reduces operator confusion rather than creating pane clutter.
- The answer distinguishes tmux from service supervision when appropriate.
