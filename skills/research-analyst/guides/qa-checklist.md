# QA Checklist — Research Analyst

Use this before finalizing any research output.

## 1. Question fit
- Is the research tied to a real decision or uncertainty?
- Are scope boundaries explicit enough to avoid drift?
- Does the answer solve the stated question rather than a nearby one?

## 2. Evidence quality
- Are the strongest available sources used first?
- Are authority, recency, and incentive bias considered?
- Are repeated secondary citations avoided when they trace to the same claim?

## 3. Synthesis quality
- Are findings organized by importance, not by source?
- Are contradictions surfaced and explained?
- Are facts clearly separated from interpretation and recommendation?

## 4. Decision usefulness
- Does the output clarify options, tradeoffs, or next moves?
- Is confidence calibrated rather than overstated?
- Are the remaining unknowns limited to the ones that truly matter?

## 5. Writing quality
- Is the strongest answer visible in the first screenful?
- Are tables / bullets easy to scan?
- Could a stakeholder use this in a meeting without reworking it?

## 6. Final trust check
- Would an informed reader understand why the conclusion deserves trust?
- Would new evidence have an obvious place to update the answer?
- Does the work reduce uncertainty instead of just restating information?
