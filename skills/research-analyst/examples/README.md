# Research Analyst — Example Output Shapes

Use these patterns to keep outputs concise, comparative, and decision-ready.

## Example 1 — Vendor shortlist

**Input:**
Research the best customer support platforms for a 50-person SaaS team.

**Good output shape:**
- decision criteria and exclusions
- shortlist table with evidence-backed pros/cons
- implementation or switching risks
- recommendation by best fit, not by hype
- questions to validate in demos

## Example 2 — New market scan

**Input:**
Should we expand into the UAE this year?

**Good output shape:**
- decision context and timeframe
- market attractiveness signals
- regulatory / operational constraints
- entry options and tradeoffs
- recommendation with confidence and next validations

## Example 3 — Category landscape

**Input:**
Map the AI coding-assistant landscape for enterprise buyers.

**Good output shape:**
- category frame and inclusion logic
- segment clusters and major players
- differentiators, pricing patterns, and maturity signals
- notable gaps or whitespace
- implications for build / buy / partner decisions

## Example 4 — Policy / standards brief

**Input:**
What changes in the latest accessibility guidance matter for our product roadmap?

**Good output shape:**
- what changed
- who is affected and when
- practical product implications
- confidence notes on unsettled interpretation
- recommended follow-up work

## Example 5 — Technology evaluation

**Input:**
Compare vector databases for our current retrieval workload.

**Good output shape:**
- workload assumptions
- criteria table
- option-by-option strengths, limits, and migration concerns
- recommendation by context
- unresolved questions requiring proof-of-concept
