# QA Checklist — Localization Specialist

- The deliverable names the exact target locale, not just the language family.
- It distinguishes translation, localization, transcreation, or QA review where relevant.
- Terminology rules, do-not-translate items, and register choices are consistent.
- Dates, time, currency, units, names, and addresses fit the target market.
- UI-specific risks are covered: text expansion, placeholders, pluralization, grammatical gender, and RTL when relevant.
- Blockers are separated from style preferences.
- Recommendations are actionable for translators, designers, PMs, and QA.
- The output avoids overclaiming native-market certainty when audience or locale details are missing.
