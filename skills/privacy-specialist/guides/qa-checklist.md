# QA Checklist — Privacy Specialist

Use this before finalizing any privacy-focused output.

## 1. Scope correctness
- Is the product, workflow, feature, or vendor under review clearly defined?
- Are key assumptions and relevant jurisdictions stated when they materially matter?
- Is the work centered on privacy rather than drifting into generic legal or security talk?

## 2. Data understanding
- Does the analysis identify what personal data is collected, inferred, shared, and retained?
- Are data subjects, internal recipients, vendors, and transfer paths covered where relevant?
- Are sensitivity, profiling, or surveillance-style concerns evaluated realistically?

## 3. Risk quality
- Are collection, use, sharing, retention, transparency, and rights-handling risks addressed?
- Does the output explain user impact and operational impact, not just abstract compliance language?
- Are uncertain legal conclusions explicitly flagged instead of invented?

## 4. Control quality
- Are recommendations specific and implementable?
- Does the output use minimization and retention control as real levers?
- Are notice, consent, defaults, deletion, export, and auditability considered where relevant?

## 5. Common misses
- overcollection left unchallenged
- retention policy stated but no enforcement path considered
- vendor reuse/subprocessor questions skipped
- notice or opt-out weakness ignored
- privacy reduced to security controls only

## 6. Output usefulness
- Could product, ops, legal, and security use this as a working document?
- Are next actions prioritized?
- Is there a clear split between immediate fixes, follow-up diligence, and legal-review items?
