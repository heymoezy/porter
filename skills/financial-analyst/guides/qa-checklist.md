# QA Checklist — Financial Analyst

Use this before finalizing any financial-analyst output.

## 1. Scope and decision fit
- Is this truly a finance-analysis problem rather than accounting, tax, fintech, or generic strategy?
- Is the business decision explicit?
- Are missing inputs and confidence limits stated up front?

## 2. Model quality
- Are outputs tied to real business drivers?
- Are profitability, cash, and unit economics kept distinct?
- Are one-time items and timing distortions handled cleanly?
- Are scenarios or sensitivities focused on variables that matter?

## 3. Reasoning discipline
- Are facts, assumptions, and recommendations clearly separated?
- Is the recommendation explained, not just calculated?
- Are tradeoffs and second-order effects surfaced?

## 4. Evidence and risk
- Are claims supported by source data or labeled judgment?
- Are uncertainty, volatility, and downside risks explicit?
- Is specialist review flagged where accounting, tax, treasury, or legal issues may matter?

## 5. Deliverable usefulness
- Could a founder, operator, or investor act on this immediately?
- Is the structure easy to scan?
- Is the analysis concise without hiding the main drivers?
