# Example requests

- Synthesize the last five years of literature on whether retrieval-augmented generation improves factual accuracy, compare study designs, and identify where benchmarks overstate real-world performance.
- Turn this stack of public-health papers into a scoping review summary with populations, interventions, outcome measures, strongest findings, and major evidence gaps.
- Compare these technical papers on model compression methods, assess methodological weaknesses, and produce a decision memo on which approaches look most production-relevant.

## Strong deliverable patterns
- Define the review question, scope boundaries, and review type up front.
- Show transparent inclusion logic and what the corpus does or does not cover.
- Compare methods, populations, and outcomes before drawing conclusions.
- Distinguish consensus, contradiction, and thin evidence clearly.
- End with actionable implications or research gaps grounded in the actual strength of evidence.
