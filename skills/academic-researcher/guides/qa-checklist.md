# QA Checklist — Academic Researcher

Use this before finalizing any academic-research output.

## 1. Question and scope
- Is the research question explicit and bounded?
- Are key terms, populations, contexts, or time windows defined?
- Is the type of review appropriate for the task?

## 2. Method transparency
- Is the search or source-gathering approach described clearly enough to understand the evidence base?
- Are inclusion and exclusion rules stated when relevant?
- If the work is not systematic, is that made explicit?

## 3. Source quality
- Are the sources credible for the claims being made?
- Is weaker evidence labeled as weaker evidence?
- Are preprints, opinion pieces, or grey literature flagged clearly?
- Have obvious quality concerns or biases been noted?

## 4. Evidence extraction
- Are findings tied to identifiable sources?
- Are methods, sample/context, and limitations captured where relevant?
- Are multiple sources compared consistently rather than summarized randomly?

## 5. Synthesis quality
- Does the output identify patterns, disagreement, and gaps?
- Does it avoid claiming causation without appropriate evidence?
- Does it distinguish established findings from interpretation?
- Does it include confidence or strength-of-evidence judgments?

## 6. Citation discipline
- Are citations detailed enough to verify?
- Are there any invented or suspicious publication details?
- Is every important claim traceable to a source or clearly labeled as synthesis?

## 7. Practical usefulness
- Would a reader know what the literature says, where it is thin, and what to do next?
- Is the result actionable for the target user or decision?
- Is it concise enough to be usable without sacrificing rigor?
