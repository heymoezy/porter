# Partnership Developer — Example Output Shapes

Use these as patterns for partnership-focused outputs.

## Example 1 — Prioritized target shortlist

**Input:**
Find likely partnership targets for our B2B payroll software.

**Good output shape:**
| Partner type | Example target profile | Why it fits | Value to them | Complexity | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| HRIS platforms | mid-market HR systems | shared buyer and workflow adjacency | more payroll flexibility for customers | Medium | High |
| Accounting firms | payroll-adjacent advisors | trusted SMB channel | referral revenue + stickier client service | Low | High |
| Benefits brokers | SMB benefits agencies | same buyer moment | broader offer to retain accounts | Medium | Medium |

Then add:
- recommended first outreach motion
- disqualifiers
- likely pilot scope

## Example 2 — Partnership thesis

**Input:**
Help us build a partnership strategy for entering the education market.

**Good output shape:**
- objective and why partnership is the right lever
- priority partner archetypes
- mutual value by archetype
- first-wave targets
- pilot design and success metrics
- internal readiness gaps

## Example 3 — Outreach / meeting narrative

**Input:**
Draft the narrative for a co-marketing plus integration partnership pitch.

**Good output shape:**
- why the overlap matters now
- user problem solved together
- proposed first collaboration
- traction or proof to cite
- clear ask
- low-friction next step

## Example 4 — Deal-model comparison

**Input:**
Should this be referral, reseller, or strategic alliance?

**Good output shape:**
| Model | Best when | Upside | Main risk | Operational requirement |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Referral | low-touch lead sharing is enough | fast to launch | weak control over follow-through | attribution + payout rules |
| Reseller | partner can sell and support | larger distribution leverage | enablement burden | pricing, training, support model |
| Strategic alliance | multi-layer objective exists | wider ecosystem leverage | vague accountability | executive sponsor + governance |
