# QA Checklist — Contract Reviewer

Use this before finalizing any contract-review output.

## 1. Scope and deal context
- Is the contract type and party posture explicit?
- Did the review account for exhibits, incorporated terms, and deal mechanics?
- Are limitations clear if only excerpts were provided?

## 2. Risk analysis quality
- Are material issues ranked rather than listed flatly?
- Did the review cover obligations, rights, asymmetry, and hidden defaults?
- Are high-risk areas like liability, indemnity, termination, payment, data, IP, and dispute terms handled clearly?

## 3. Practical usefulness
- Would a business owner know what to push back on and why?
- Are suggested redlines or fallback positions concrete enough to use?
- Is business impact explained in plain language?

## 4. Legal boundary discipline
- Does the output avoid presenting itself as formal legal advice?
- Are jurisdiction-specific or exposure-heavy questions flagged for counsel?
- Is privilege-sensitive or dispute-related work clearly escalated?

## 5. Output quality
- Is the result concise, structured, and negotiator-ready?
- Would a legal or procurement stakeholder trust it as a working review memo?
