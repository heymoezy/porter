# QA Checklist — Due Diligence Analyst

Run this before finalizing any diligence output.

## 1. Decision fit
- Is the actual decision explicit?
- Is the review scope and its limit clear?
- Would a reader know what still needs specialist diligence?

## 2. Evidence discipline
- Are major claims tested against evidence instead of repeated?
- Are fact, inference, and assumption separated cleanly?
- Are data gaps, contradictions, and confidence limits visible?

## 3. Risk quality
- Are risks ranked by materiality rather than listed flatly?
- Are concentration, dependency, and fragility examined?
- Are reversible issues separated from thesis-breaking ones?

## 4. Commercial usefulness
- Does each issue explain economic or strategic consequence?
- Are the highest-value next diligence asks specific?
- Would the recommendation help with pricing, structure, timing, or go / no-go?

## 5. Judgment quality
- Is the recommendation decisive enough to act on?
- Is confidence proportionate to the evidence base?
- Does the output avoid false precision and false balance?

## 6. Communication quality
- Are the top red flags easy to find in seconds?
- Is the writing concise, senior, and scannable?
- Would the memo change how a decision-maker proceeds?
