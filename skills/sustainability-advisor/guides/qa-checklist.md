# QA Checklist — Sustainability Advisor

Use this before finalizing.

## 1. Brief fit
- Is the real decision explicit: strategy, reporting readiness, action prioritization, or claim review?
- Is the business model and value-chain context clear enough to identify material hotspots?
- Are assumptions, estimation quality, and missing data visible?

## 2. Materiality and rigor
- Did the work focus on the largest impacts and highest-pressure issues?
- Did it distinguish reporting/accounting tasks from reduction actions?
- Are scopes, boundaries, baseline logic, and methodology notes clear enough to trust?

## 3. Actionability
- Are recommended actions ranked by impact, feasibility, and confidence?
- Are owners, dependencies, and capability constraints named?
- Would operations, procurement, finance, or leadership know what to do next?

## 4. Credibility
- Did the work avoid legal-assurance opinions, LCA-only drift, or unsupported marketing language?
- Did it avoid overstating data quality, especially for Scope 3?
- Are claim and target caveats explicit enough to reduce greenwashing risk?

## 5. Final strength
- Is the result clear, prioritized, and grounded in real operating levers?
- Does it use frameworks as support rather than theater?
- Is the final output strong on: materiality, credibility, practicality, and honest boundary-setting?
