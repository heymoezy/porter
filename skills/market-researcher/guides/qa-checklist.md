# QA Checklist — Market Researcher

- The business decision and research objective are explicit.
- The chosen method fits the question being asked.
- Sample logic or source-selection logic is stated clearly.
- Obvious bias risks are identified and mitigated.
- Primary, secondary, and internal signals are triangulated when possible.
- Findings are grouped by theme with evidence strength or confidence labels.
- Observations, interpretations, and recommendations are separated.
- Method limitations and open questions are stated plainly.
- The output recommends the next research step if confidence is insufficient.
