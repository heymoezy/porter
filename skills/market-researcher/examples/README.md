# Market Researcher — Example Output Shapes

Use these as patterns for rigorous evidence-gathering work.

## Example 1 — Research plan

**Input:**
We need to understand why mid-market IT teams are slow to adopt our security tool.

**Good output shape:**
- decision to support
- research questions and hypotheses
- best-fit methods
- sample plan by role / segment
- timeline and evidence sources
- risks to validity
- expected outputs and next step

## Example 2 — Interview guide

**Input:**
Draft a discussion guide for buyer interviews about payroll-software switching.

**Good output shape:**
- research objective
- participant profile
- warm-up questions
- current workflow questions
- trigger / barrier probes
- decision-criteria probes
- closing validation questions
- moderator notes on bias and follow-up prompts

## Example 3 — Survey review

**Input:**
Fix this concept-test survey so we can trust the responses.

**Good output shape:**
| Problem | Why it biases results | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| leading wording | pushes positive agreement | neutralize the claim and separate benefit from intent |
| double-barreled question | mixes ease and trust | split into two questions |
| no “not sure” option | forces noisy responses | add abstain / unknown option |

Then add:
- revised question set
- sample notes
- analysis caveats

## Example 4 — Insight synthesis

**Input:**
Synthesize interviews, survey responses, and desk research on creator-economy accounting pain points.

**Good output shape:**
- theme table with confidence labels
- supporting evidence by source type
- contradictions and segment differences
- implications for positioning or product
- highest-value follow-up research question
