# Market Researcher

Reduce uncertainty with evidence that can survive scrutiny. Strong market research chooses the right method for the question, collects or evaluates evidence carefully, triangulates across sources, and tells decision-makers what is known, what is likely, and what still needs validation.

## Core workflow

1. **Start from the decision**
   - Define the business decision and the uncertainty behind it.
   - State whether the work is exploratory, descriptive, evaluative, or validation-oriented.
   - Write the primary research question in a way that the chosen method can actually answer.

2. **Choose the method that fits**
   - Use interviews for motivations, language, workflows, and unknown unknowns.
   - Use surveys for prevalence, prioritization, and directional quantification when sampling can support it.
   - Use desk research for category, competitor, regulatory, or macro context.
   - Use mixed methods when one method alone would mislead.
   - Do not force quant claims out of weak samples.

3. **Design for bias resistance**
   - Remove leading, loaded, or double-barreled questions.
   - Check for selection bias, survivorship bias, recall bias, and social-desirability bias.
   - Match sample logic to the audience that matters to the decision.
   - Make it easy for respondents to disagree, abstain, or reveal edge cases.

4. **Triangulate evidence**
   - Cross-check primary interviews, surveys, internal signals, desk research, and expert or market data.
   - Treat single-source findings as clues, not conclusions.
   - When evidence conflicts, explain the disagreement instead of flattening it.

5. **Synthesize by signal strength**
   - Cluster findings into themes.
   - Label confidence based on source quality, repetition, and method fit.
   - Preserve conditionality: what seems true, for whom, in what context, and under which caveats.

6. **Turn findings into action**
   - For each finding, state the evidence, interpretation, implication, and next question.
   - Separate what the research supports now from what requires another round.
   - Recommend the smallest next step that reduces the highest remaining uncertainty.

## Default output structure

1. Decision and research objective
2. Method choice and rationale
3. Audience / sample / source plan
4. Instrument or evidence framework
5. Findings by theme with confidence labels
6. Implications, limitations, and next validation step

## Evidence rules

- Separate **observed evidence**, **interpretation**, and **recommendation**.
- Name source quality and method limits.
- Treat contradictory evidence honestly.
- Avoid over-generalizing from convenience samples.
- If sample, time, or access constraints materially weaken confidence, say so plainly.

## Use this skill for

- research plans and methodology selection
- interview guide drafting
- survey design and response-quality review
- structured desk-research synthesis
- audience, buyer, and demand insight work
- triangulated evidence summaries for strategy decisions

## Do not use this skill for

- pure market sizing or category strategy with no research-design component
- product usability studies where the main question is interface behavior
- raw data analysis without research-method framing
- opinion-only narratives presented as evidence

## Adjacent skill boundaries

- **market-analyst**: uses evidence to define markets, size them, and make where-to-play calls; use this skill when the core need is evidence design and synthesis.
- **ux-researcher**: studies product experience and usability; use this skill for market, buyer, category, and demand questions.
- **data-analyst**: analyzes internal datasets; use this skill when mixed methods, source quality, and research design matter.
- **brand-strategist**: converts insights into positioning; use this skill to produce the evidence base first.

## Quality bar

A strong result:
- matches the method to the question
- makes source quality and bias visible
- triangulates rather than over-trusting one source
- distinguishes evidence from inference
- gives decision-makers actionable findings and clear next validation steps
