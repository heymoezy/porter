# QA Checklist — Logistics Optimizer

- The answer names the dominant logistics constraint rather than just the visible symptom.
- Tradeoffs among service level, cost, capacity, and resilience are explicit.
- Recommendations respect warehouse, inventory, transportation, labor, and carrier realities.
- Structural fixes are separated from tactical workarounds.
- Each major recommendation is tied to customer-facing and efficiency KPIs.
- The sequence of actions is clear: now, next, later.
- The advice avoids finance-only optimization detached from real goods flow.
- The output is actionable for operators, planners, and leadership.
