# QA Checklist — Social Media Manager

Use this before finalizing social strategy work.

## 1. Objective and audience fit
- Is the business goal explicit?
- Are the audience segments, funnel stage, or community context clear enough?
- Are assumptions stated where evidence is weak?

## 2. Channel-role quality
- Does each platform have a distinct role?
- Are formats and post ideas native to the channel instead of copy-pasted?
- Does the plan avoid treating every metric as equally valuable?

## 3. System design and actionability
- Are the pillars, recurring formats, or calendar specific enough to produce?
- Is cadence realistic for the available team, approvals, and asset capacity?
- Would the next operator know what to publish, when, and why?

## 4. Community and risk handling
- Are comments, DMs, complaints, or escalation paths covered when relevant?
- Does the work protect voice, trust, and compliance constraints?
- Are creator or partner guardrails clear when collaboration is involved?

## 5. Measurement quality
- Are the success signals tied to the actual objective?
- Does the analysis distinguish meaningful engagement from vanity metrics?
- Are scale / cut / test recommendations clear?

## 6. Writing quality
- Is the output concise, specific, and free of generic social-media clichés?
- Would a strong marketing operator trust this as execution-ready guidance?
- Does it meet the quality bar: goal alignment, platform-native thinking, repeatable system design, audience relevance, actionable iteration?
