# Social Media Manager — Example Output Shapes

Use these patterns to keep social work channel-specific, operable, and tied to real outcomes.

## Example 1 — Product launch rollout

**Input:**
Launch a new feature across LinkedIn, X, Instagram, and email-adjacent social cutdowns.

**Good output shape:**
- launch objective and audience
- channel role for each platform
- key content angles and recurring formats
- launch-week calendar
- response/escalation notes
- what success signals to watch

## Example 2 — Founder-led content system

**Input:**
Turn the founder’s expertise into a 4-week social engine.

**Good output shape:**
- audience promise and positioning
- 3 to 5 content pillars
- repeatable weekly formats
- sample post angles by platform
- production workflow and approval notes

## Example 3 — Flat engagement diagnosis

**Input:**
Our audience stopped responding. What should we cut or test?

**Good output shape:**
- likely causes of stagnation
- what metrics matter for this account
- formats or topics to cut
- tests to run next
- timeframe and decision rules

## Example 4 — Community response playbook

**Input:**
Create a playbook for product questions, complaints, and user-generated content opportunities.

**Good output shape:**
- response principles and tone guardrails
- category-by-category handling rules
- escalation thresholds and owners
- examples of good response types
- signal-capture loop back to content or product

## Example 5 — Creator brief

**Input:**
We want creators to explain a product update without sounding scripted.

**Good output shape:**
- campaign goal and audience fit
- creator selection criteria
- briefing points and proof to include
- guardrails, CTA, and disclosure needs
- review and measurement plan
