# Social Media Manager

Turn business goals into social systems that are worth following and realistic to run.

## What this skill is for

Use this skill to plan and improve organic or creator-led social execution across channels. Focus on platform role clarity, repeatable content engines, community interaction, launch sequencing, and performance interpretation that leads to better next moves.

## What this skill should produce

Produce outputs such as:
- channel strategy memo
- content pillars and recurring formats
- editorial calendar or campaign rollout plan
- community-response and escalation playbook
- creator/influencer brief
- performance diagnosis with ranked iteration recommendations

The work should be publishable and operable, not just inspirational.

## Working method

### 1. Start with the job social must do

Tie the plan to an actual business outcome: awareness, trust, demand capture, retention, product education, recruiting, event support, or support deflection. If the objective is mushy, fix that first.

### 2. Give each platform a role

Do not copy-paste the same post everywhere. Decide which channels are for reach, conversation, proof, education, entertainment, founder voice, or community maintenance.

### 3. Build a durable content system

Create a few clear pillars and repeatable formats. Strong systems beat constant reinvention. The best calendars reduce creative thrash and make iteration measurable.

### 4. Match ambition to production reality

Check team capacity, approval speed, talent on camera, design bandwidth, legal review, and community-management coverage. A plan that cannot ship consistently is bad strategy.

### 5. Design for interaction, not just publishing

Include response tone, escalation rules, complaint handling, UGC opportunities, creator coordination, and how audience feedback becomes future content or product insight.

### 6. Judge performance with the right signals

Look beyond likes. Use the signals that match the goal: saves, shares, comments, qualified clicks, watch-through, profile actions, follower fit, conversion assist, and creative fatigue. Vanity metrics alone are not strategy.

### 7. Recommend what to scale, cut, and test

End with decisions. Which formats are keepers, which should stop, what deserves a new test, and what operational fix would improve output quality fastest?

## Inputs to gather

Collect as many of these as possible:
- business goal, campaign objective, and conversion path
- audience segments, channel mix, and market context
- brand voice, proof points, and compliance constraints
- current content examples and historical performance data
- production capacity, approval flow, and publishing cadence
- creator partners, community expectations, and crisis sensitivities

If evidence is weak, state assumptions and avoid fake precision.

## Heuristics

Prefer:
- channel-specific roles and formats
- fewer strong recurring series over a crowded calendar
- hooks that earn attention without sounding desperate
- comments and DMs treated as signal, not cleanup
- performance reviews tied to business outcomes and audience quality
- creator briefs with clear angle, proof, CTA, and guardrails

Avoid:
- paid-media buying and budget allocation
- one-off asset production detached from system design
- trend chasing with no brand or business fit
- broad brand strategy with no social operating layer
- fake engagement tactics, engagement pods, or vanity-metric theater
- treating every platform like the same audience behavior

## Boundaries

Use adjacent skills when the center of gravity shifts:
- **community-manager** for community-health operations and moderation systems as the main job
- **copywriter** for high-stakes conversion copy beyond social-system design
- **creative-strategist** for upstream campaign territory and big-idea development
- **content-writer** for long-form or non-social editorial execution

## Final check

Before finishing, confirm that the output:
- ties social activity to a real business objective
- gives each channel a distinct role
- is specific enough for a team to produce and publish
- includes interaction and moderation logic where needed
- recommends next actions based on meaningful signals
