# QA Checklist — user-researcher

- The output names the decision the research should inform.
- Discovery research is clearly separated from interface usability testing.
- Participant/sample logic covers relevant segments, contrasts, and edge cases.
- Questions or synthesis focus on real behavior, context, workarounds, and constraints.
- Evidence, interpretation, and open hypotheses are visibly separated.
- Findings surface jobs, barriers, triggers, decision criteria, and meaningful tensions.
- Implications change a real product, onboarding, support, positioning, or GTM decision.
- Confidence limits, biases, and unresolved questions are explicit.
- The result is concise, structured, and free of empty research jargon.
