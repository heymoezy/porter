# Examples — user-researcher

## Representative requests

### 1. Discovery plan for churn
“Build a discovery research plan to understand why teams activate but churn before month two.”

Expected shape:
- define the churn decision and what Porter needs to learn
- specify participant groups, contrasts, and recruiting logic
- outline an interview guide focused on recent behavior and switching forces
- show the synthesis frame and decision outputs

### 2. Evidence synthesis
“Synthesize our interview notes, support tickets, and sales calls into jobs, blockers, and segment differences.”

Expected shape:
- separate evidence from inference
- cluster recurring jobs, triggers, barriers, and workarounds
- highlight contradictions and thin-evidence areas
- translate the synthesis into product and messaging implications

### 3. Workflow understanding
“Help us understand how operations teams currently manage approvals across email, Slack, and spreadsheets.”

Expected shape:
- map the current workflow, actors, handoffs, and hidden work
- identify why the current workaround persists
- surface trust, compliance, or coordination constraints
- suggest what to test or build next

## Output expectation
A strong answer should:
- uncover what people are actually trying to achieve in context
- make meaningful segment differences and unmet needs visible
- avoid shallow theme lists and decorative personas
- end with decisions, tradeoffs, and concrete next moves
