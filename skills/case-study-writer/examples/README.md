# Case Study Writer — Example Output Shapes

Use these as patterns for strong case-study deliverables.

## Example 1 — Long-form customer story

**Input:**
Write a website case study for a logistics platform that cut invoice-processing time by 65 percent.

**Good output shape:**
- headline with outcome
- customer background and relevance
- challenge and stakes
- solution and rollout summary
- measurable results with timeframe
- customer quote and closing takeaway

## Example 2 — Interview synthesis

**Input:**
Turn this customer interview transcript into a polished case study draft.

**Good output shape:**
- extracted proof points and missing-info flags
- narrative draft in challenge-solution-results order
- quote candidates
- claims that need fact-check or approval
- optional shorter abstract for homepage use

## Example 3 — Anonymous enterprise case study

**Input:**
Create an anonymized case study for a regulated healthcare client.

**Good output shape:**
- anonymized customer description
- challenge framed without exposing sensitive data
- compliant explanation of the solution
- approved results and guarded wording
- notes on what cannot be claimed publicly
- version for sales-only sharing if needed

## Example 4 — Sales enablement one-pager

**Input:**
Condense our customer story into something reps can send after discovery calls.

**Good output shape:**
- short positioning summary
- customer challenge snapshot
- 3 to 5 proof points
- one quote or testimonial excerpt
- why this story matters for similar buyers
- suggested CTA or follow-up use

## Example 5 — Multi-case comparison

**Input:**
Summarize three related customer wins for a vertical campaign.

**Good output shape:**
- audience and campaign angle
- each customer mini-story in consistent format
- pattern across the stories
- strongest shared proof points
- claims or segments to avoid overgeneralizing
