# QA Checklist — Case Study Writer

Use this before finalizing any case-study output.

## 1. Story integrity
- Is the customer context clear enough to matter to the target audience?
- Is the challenge concrete and believable?
- Does the story follow a clean challenge-solution-results flow?

## 2. Proof quality
- Are metrics, timeframe, and attribution explicit where claims are made?
- Are quotes real, useful, and placed for credibility?
- Are weak or missing proof points flagged instead of inflated?

## 3. Audience usefulness
- Would a buyer quickly understand why this story is relevant?
- Is the vendor positioned as the enabler rather than the hero?
- Is the level of technical or business detail right for the channel?

## 4. Compliance and sensitivity
- Are naming, anonymization, and approval constraints respected?
- Are regulated or high-risk claims worded carefully?
- Are unsupported statements removed or qualified?

## 5. Overall strength
- Does the case study feel credible rather than promotional?
- Does it deliver reusable proof for marketing or sales teams?
- Would a skeptical reader believe the result and remember the story?
