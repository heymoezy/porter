# QA Checklist — Go-to-Market Planner

## 1. Market clarity
- Is the target customer concrete, narrow enough, and commercially meaningful?
- Is the buying trigger or urgent problem explicit?

## 2. Positioning quality
- Does the plan state who it is for, what it solves, what it replaces, and why it wins?
- Are proof points or reasons to believe present?

## 3. Motion design
- Do channels and sales motion fit how this buyer actually discovers, evaluates, and buys?
- Is the conversion path explicit rather than implied?

## 4. Execution realism
- Are phases, owners, dependencies, and readiness gaps named clearly?
- Would product, sales, marketing, and support know their role?

## 5. Validation discipline
- Are major assumptions surfaced?
- Are there experiments or signals that would confirm or disprove the plan quickly?

## 6. Metric usefulness
- Are success metrics tied to adoption, pipeline, conversion, retention, or revenue rather than vanity stats?

## 7. Finish quality
- Is the output action-oriented instead of slide-deck fluff?
- Does it make the next GTM decision easier?
