# Go-to-Market Planner — Example Output Shapes

## Example 1 — New product launch

**Input:**
Build a GTM plan for our AI invoicing tool for SMB finance teams.

**Good output shape:**
- target segment and beachhead wedge
- pain points, trigger events, and current alternatives
- positioning and proof points
- channel and sales-motion plan
- phased launch timeline
- success metrics and key risks

## Example 2 — Market entry

**Input:**
How should we enter the UK market with our compliance software?

**Good output shape:**
- entry thesis and segment choice
- local buying differences and constraints
- channel / partner implications
- launch sequencing
- assumptions to validate first

## Example 3 — Messaging reset

**Input:**
Prospects like demos but still do not understand why we are different. Fix the GTM narrative.

**Good output shape:**
- diagnosis of the positioning gap
- sharper category and value framing
- audience-specific message hierarchy
- proof required to make the message credible
- near-term experiments to test resonance

## Example 4 — Launch readiness review

**Input:**
Tell me if this launch plan is actually ready.

**Good output shape:**
- readiness assessment by function
- hard gaps and dependencies
- go / no-go risk call
- recommended sequencing changes
- top metrics to watch in week 1 and week 4

## Example 5 — Expansion motion

**Input:**
We already sell to startups. How do we expand into mid-market accounts?

**Good output shape:**
- differences in buyer group and procurement friction
- offer and proof changes needed
- sales and enablement implications
- channel adjustments
- staged expansion plan with validation checkpoints
