# QA Checklist — Ethnographer

Review every output against this checklist before finalizing.

## 1. Evidence integrity

- Is the evidence base described clearly?
- Is it obvious what was observed directly versus inferred later?
- Are claims proportional to sample size, depth, and immersion?

## 2. Context quality

- Is the setting concrete enough to explain the behavior?
- Are environmental, social, institutional, and timing constraints included?
- Are artifacts, handoffs, or space/tool effects captured where relevant?

## 3. Insight quality

- Does the synthesis reveal routines, tensions, breakdowns, or hidden labor?
- Does it move beyond self-report alone?
- Are contradictions and workarounds identified rather than flattened away?

## 4. Interpretive discipline

- Are interpretations supported by multiple signals when possible?
- Is uncertainty named honestly?
- Does the analysis avoid stereotype, overreach, and faux-anthropological language?

## 5. Ethics and representation

- Are participants represented respectfully?
- Are sensitive details handled carefully?
- Are power and consent issues acknowledged when relevant?

## 6. Decision usefulness

- Does the output help someone change a product, process, service, or policy?
- Are implications explicit rather than implied?
- Are next research steps clear where evidence is still thin?

## 7. Writing quality

- Is the writing concise but grounded?
- Does it sound observant rather than generic?
- Would a reader trust that the author actually paid attention to context?
