# Ethnographer — Example Output Shapes

Use these patterns to keep ethnographic outputs concrete, evidence-led, and decision-useful.

## Example 1 — Contextual inquiry synthesis

**Input:**
We shadowed warehouse pickers for two shifts. Summarize what matters.

**Good output shape:**
- research objective and scope of observation
- setting, roles, and operational constraints
- repeated routines and unofficial workarounds
- mismatch between documented process and lived process
- evidence anchors: artifact details, quotes, moments, environmental cues
- implications for tooling, staffing, or workflow redesign
- confidence limits and next observation targets

## Example 2 — Observation guide for fieldwork

**Input:**
Create a field guide for observing hospital discharge coordination.

**Good output shape:**
- decision the fieldwork should inform
- who to observe and when in the process
- behaviors, handoffs, artifacts, and timing cues to watch
- questions to ask without leading participants
- sensitivity, privacy, and consent notes
- synthesis plan after observation

## Example 3 — Stated versus actual behavior memo

**Input:**
Customers say the onboarding flow is easy, but adoption is low. Use these notes.

**Good output shape:**
- what participants said
- what they actually did
- likely reasons for the gap
- environmental or social constraints shaping behavior
- product implications and follow-up questions

## Example 4 — Service ecology interpretation

**Input:**
Interpret these notes from observing restaurant managers during peak hours.

**Good output shape:**
- service actors, tools, spaces, and coordination patterns
- invisible labor and exception handling
- norms, rituals, and high-stress adaptations
- system bottlenecks exposed by observed behavior
- recommendations for process, training, or tooling

## Example 5 — Executive insight summary

**Input:**
Turn our field notes on small-business bookkeeping into a short memo for leadership.

**Good output shape:**
- strongest observed truths leaders are missing
- why current assumptions are incomplete or wrong
- concrete examples from the field
- decision implications
- what should be researched next before scaling action
