# QA Checklist — Quality Reviewer

## 1. Review framing
- Is the artifact under review explicit?
- Is the audience, use case, and approval/release context clear?
- Are acceptance criteria stated or reasonably inferred?
- Is the verdict visible early?

## 2. Evidence discipline
- Does every important finding point to evidence, examples, or observable gaps?
- Are factual issues separated from speculative risks?
- Are recommendations tied directly to the issue they address?

## 3. Severity calibration
- Are blockers separated from major, minor, and polish items?
- Does severity reflect real impact rather than personal preference?
- Is urgency distinguished from severity where useful?

## 4. Actionability
- Does each issue explain why it matters?
- Can the owner act on the recommendation immediately?
- Are findings grouped in a way that helps sequencing fixes?

## 5. Balance and fairness
- Does the review acknowledge what is working well?
- Does it avoid taste-only nitpicks unless they affect outcomes?
- Would the owner see the review as rigorous rather than theatrical?

## 6. Final quality
- Is the output decision-ready?
- Would a stakeholder trust the verdict?
- Does the review reduce approval or release risk in a concrete way?
