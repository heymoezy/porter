# QA Checklist — Web Search

Use this before finalizing any web-research output.

## 1. Question fit
- Did the work answer the actual question, not a nearby one?
- Is the freshness requirement clear and satisfied?
- Is the output shape right for the ask: answer, memo, comparison, or source list?

## 2. Source quality
- Were the strongest available primary or authoritative sources used?
- Are sources recent enough for the topic?
- Were high-stakes or disputed claims corroborated?

## 3. Citation integrity
- Are source names, titles, and links accurate?
- Can a reader verify the important claims quickly?
- Are citations attached to the claims that matter most?

## 4. Reasoning discipline
- Are facts separated from interpretation?
- Are caveats, conflicts, and unknowns stated clearly?
- Did the output avoid confidence laundering from weak evidence?

## 5. Synthesis quality
- Does the result rank or explain source strength?
- Does it summarize what matters instead of listing links?
- Would a decision-maker know what is confirmed, likely, and unresolved?

## 6. Efficiency
- Was the search purposeful rather than repetitive?
- Did the work avoid unnecessary breadth?
- Is the final answer concise enough to be useful quickly?
