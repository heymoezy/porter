# QA Checklist — Dashboard Designer

- The primary user, job, and decision are explicit.
- The dashboard is framed as operational, analytical, or diagnostic.
- Metrics are prioritized into tiers instead of dumped into one view.
- Each recommended chart answers a specific question.
- Quantitative comparisons rely on strong encodings, not decorative ones.
- The scan path from headline KPI to next action is obvious.
- Labels, units, targets, thresholds, and date windows are defined.
- Filters, drill-downs, and annotations are specified clearly.
- Empty, loading, error, stale-data, and mobile states are covered.
- The design is concrete enough for product and engineering to implement.
