# QA Checklist — SEO Specialist

Use this before finalizing any SEO deliverable.

## 1. Intent and page fit
- Is the likely search intent stated clearly?
- Does the recommendation match the right page type for the SERP?
- Does the output avoid keyword obsession without user value?

## 2. On-page quality
- Are title, H1, structure, and content recommendations specific?
- Is the primary topic obvious quickly?
- Are content gaps, thin sections, or cannibalization risks addressed?

## 3. Technical quality
- Are crawl, render, canonical, redirect, mobile, and indexing issues considered when relevant?
- Are high-impact blockers prioritized over low-value tweaks?
- Is discoverability discussed alongside content quality?

## 4. Strategic usefulness
- Are recommendations ranked by likely impact and effort?
- Are internal-linking and site-architecture implications included?
- Would an editor, marketer, or builder know what to ship next?

## 5. Ethics and durability
- Does the output avoid manipulative tactics and ranking guarantees?
- Is the advice user-first and sustainable?
- Are data limitations stated honestly?

## 6. Writing quality
- Is the advice clear, concrete, and scan-friendly?
- Are recommendations tied to real search logic rather than vague SEO jargon?
- Would a strong growth or content lead trust this as practical guidance?
