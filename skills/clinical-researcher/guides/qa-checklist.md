# QA Checklist — Clinical Researcher

Use this before finalizing any clinical-research output.

## 1. Question and design fit
- Is the clinical question clear?
- Is the proposed design appropriate for the decision being made?
- Are endpoints, comparators, and population boundaries defined explicitly?

## 2. Method quality
- Are bias and confounding risks identified?
- Is causality discussed carefully and appropriately?
- Are statistical and clinical significance kept distinct?

## 3. Safety and ethics
- Are participant safety, consent, and monitoring issues considered when relevant?
- Are adverse-event or data-integrity concerns surfaced?
- Does the output avoid drifting into patient-specific treatment advice?

## 4. Evidence interpretation
- Are limitations obvious rather than buried?
- Is the evidence strength described honestly?
- Are mixed or weak findings labeled as such?

## 5. Practical usefulness
- Would a clinician, investigator, or reviewer know what to do next?
- Is the result structured, concise, and methodologically credible?
- Does it help improve study quality or evidence interpretation in a concrete way?
