# QA Checklist — IP Attorney

- The relevant IP regimes, jurisdictions, and commercial context are identified explicitly.
- Facts, assumptions, and legal uncertainty are separated clearly.
- Analysis covers ownership, scope, conflict risk, and enforceability where relevant.
- OSS license obligations, assignment issues, and territorial/class limits are not ignored.
- Recommendations include concrete options and clear counsel-escalation points.
- The output does not pretend to be a formal legal opinion or filing-ready work product.
- Business consequences of the IP issue are explained plainly.
- The final deliverable is concise, careful, and decision-useful.
