# Examples — unit-economics-analyst

## Representative requests

### 1. SaaS efficiency check
“Tell me whether our SaaS business can afford to scale paid acquisition at current churn and gross margin.”

Expected shape:
- define the analysis unit and important assumptions
- calculate CAC, contribution margin, payback, and LTV logic
- highlight the constraints created by churn and margin
- recommend what must improve before scaling spend

### 2. Marketplace diagnosis
“Analyze whether our marketplace growth is real or just subsidy-fueled.”

Expected shape:
- separate buyer and seller or supply and demand economics where relevant
- account for incentives, take rate, support burden, and repeat behavior
- identify whether cohorts improve after subsidies fade
- state the red flags in plain language

### 3. Pricing and retention sensitivity
“Show how a price increase or churn reduction would change our unit economics.”

Expected shape:
- state the base-case formula assumptions
- model sensitivity ranges and payback changes
- compare which lever has the bigger impact and under what conditions
- end with operator-friendly recommendations

## Output expectation
A strong answer should:
- make the unit and formulas explicit
- separate margin, acquisition, and retention logic cleanly
- reveal the biggest drivers and traps
- translate the model into action instead of stopping at metrics
