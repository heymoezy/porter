# QA Checklist — unit-economics-analyst

- The business model and unit of analysis are explicit.
- Revenue, gross margin, contribution margin, acquisition cost, and retention assumptions are clearly separated.
- Core formulas or formula logic are shown rather than implied.
- Channel, segment, or cohort blending does not hide important truths.
- The analysis distinguishes mature evidence from weak or immature data.
- Common traps such as gross-vs-net confusion, incentive distortion, and immature annualization are addressed.
- Sensitivity or scenario analysis is included when assumptions are uncertain.
- Recommendations tie directly to pricing, retention, channel mix, service burden, or spend decisions.
- The output avoids vanity-metric storytelling and false precision.
- The final result is concise, auditable, and decision-useful.
