# QA Checklist — Data Governance

- The in-scope data domains and risk context are explicit.
- Criticality and sensitivity are differentiated instead of flattened.
- Ownership, stewardship, approval, and remediation roles are clearly assigned.
- Policies are enforceable, reviewable, and tied to operating actions.
- Quality dimensions, thresholds, and escalation paths are defined where relevant.
- Metadata, lineage, access, retention, and exception handling are addressed as needed.
- Required legal, privacy, security, or audit reviews are flagged.
- The model balances protection with usability and avoids ceremonial bureaucracy.
- The rollout or operating cadence is realistic for the organization’s maturity.
- Another team could run the governance process without guessing intent.
