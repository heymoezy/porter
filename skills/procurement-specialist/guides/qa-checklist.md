# QA Checklist — Procurement Specialist

Use this before finalizing any procurement-focused output.

## 1. Requirement quality
- Is the business need clearly defined?
- Are must-haves separated from preferences?
- Are key stakeholders, operators, and approvers identified?

## 2. Decision rigor
- Are options compared using explicit criteria?
- Are weights or priorities defensible and tied to the business objective?
- Is the recommendation traceable to the evaluation logic?

## 3. Cost realism
- Does the analysis include implementation, migration, support, renewal, and adoption costs where relevant?
- Is price clearly distinguished from total cost of ownership?
- Are overage, growth, or exit costs considered?

## 4. Risk completeness
- Are privacy, security, supplier viability, lock-in, and service risks surfaced where relevant?
- Are required diligence and approval steps called out before signature?
- Are mitigations specific and actionable?

## 5. Common misses
- lowest-price bias left unchecked
- unweighted comparison presented as rigorous
- hidden implementation burden ignored
- renewal / lock-in risk missed
- recommendation given without a next step or condition set

## 6. Output usefulness
- Could a real buyer, operator, or approver use this to make a decision?
- Is there a clear recommendation rather than just a comparison table?
- Are next procurement steps stated plainly?
