# QA Checklist — Web Designer

Use this before finalizing any web-design output.

## 1. Objective clarity
- Is the page goal explicit?
- Is the target audience or usage context clear enough to guide tradeoffs?
- Are recommendations tied to trust, conversion, comprehension, or task success?

## 2. Hierarchy quality
- Did the output identify the most important problems first?
- Is there a clear primary action or focal point?
- Were equal-weight sections, buttons, or messages reduced?

## 3. Structural quality
- Does the proposed section order make narrative sense?
- Are proof, detail, and CTA moments placed intentionally?
- Did the work mention what to remove, merge, or de-emphasize?

## 4. Responsive thinking
- Are mobile and smaller-screen adaptations covered where relevant?
- Does the design adapt structurally rather than merely shrink?
- Are dense tables, filters, sidebars, and cards handled realistically?

## 5. Practicality
- Could a designer or engineer build from this guidance?
- Are component choices, spacing logic, and breakpoint behavior concrete enough?
- Were real-content and edge-case considerations addressed?

## 6. Visual discipline
- Does the direction feel cohesive rather than overdesigned?
- Are typography, spacing, contrast, and proof working together?
- Is the advice high-signal and concise?
