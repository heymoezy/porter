# Ghostwriter

Sound like the author. Do not fake the person.

## Scope

Use this skill for:
- founder and executive ghostwriting
- thought-leadership drafts and rewrites
- internal memos in a leader’s voice
- speeches, talking points, and keynote sections
- posts, threads, newsletters, and letters in a defined voice
- translating generic copy into a recognizably stronger point of view
- building a usable voice brief from writing samples before drafting

## Do not use this skill for

Do not use this skill for:
- fraud, spoofing, phishing, or deceptive impersonation
- invented anecdotes, credentials, opinions, or lived experience
- overclaiming expertise the named voice cannot support
- legal, medical, financial, or other regulated assertions without careful validation
- parody-style mimicry when the job is credible authorship

## Inputs to gather

Before drafting, collect:
- who the voice belongs to and what authority they actually have
- audience, channel, and desired outcome
- source samples: writing, transcripts, speeches, interviews, posts, or memos
- recurring language patterns, sentence rhythm, structure habits, and rhetorical moves
- topics the author owns strongly versus topics requiring restraint
- must-include facts, claims, and positioning
- what requires explicit approval from the named person

## Output expectations

Return outputs such as:
- a voice brief with specific rules
- a ghostwritten draft in the target voice
- a rewrite of existing content into that voice
- several channel-calibrated variants
- a list of claims, anecdotes, or specifics needing validation

## Working method

### 1. Build the voice from evidence

Extract patterns from real material:
- cadence: clipped, reflective, punchy, expansive
- diction: plainspoken, technical, metaphor-heavy, operator-like
- posture: teacher, builder, critic, evangelist, pragmatist
- structure: story-first, thesis-first, list-first, aphorism-first
- signature habits: contrasts, rhetorical questions, one-line reframes, humor, examples

Do not infer the voice from job title alone.

### 2. Lock the communication goal

Style is downstream of intent. Decide what the piece must make the audience:
- understand
- believe
- feel
- or do

If the message goal is fuzzy, the voice work will feel like costume.

### 3. Adapt the voice to the channel

The same person sounds different in:
- a board memo
- a keynote
- a LinkedIn post
- a customer letter
- an internal note

Preserve worldview and cadence while changing compression, polish, and structure to fit the medium.

### 4. Use selective signature markers

Usually one or two recognizable habits are enough. Too many turns the draft into imitation theater.

### 5. Protect factual and ethical integrity

If a claim, anecdote, or emotional stance has no support, either:
- remove it
- soften it
- mark it for approval
- or replace it with something true and defensible

### 6. Leave an approval trail

Flag anything the named author should confirm, especially:
- statistics
- personal stories
- controversial opinions
- promises
- references to people, customers, or events

## Heuristics

Prefer:
- voice fidelity grounded in samples
- substance before style mimicry
- plausible authorship over perfect imitation
- audience-aware structure
- explicit validation flags for risky specifics

Avoid:
- caricature
- generic prose with a few branded words sprinkled in
- invented intimacy or confidence
- sounding more like the ghostwriter than the named author
- confusing elegance with authenticity

## Adjacent skill boundaries

- **blog-writer**: use when the main job is article quality rather than voice match
- **copywriter**: use when persuasion and conversion are primary
- **speech-writer**: use when spoken cadence and live delivery dominate
- **brand-strategist**: use when defining a reusable brand voice system across teams and assets

## Quality bar

A strong result should:
- feel plausible to someone who knows the speaker
- preserve the speaker’s intent without parody
- fit the medium and audience
- keep facts and claims defensible
- make approval easy by clearly flagging what needs confirmation
