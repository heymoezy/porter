# QA Checklist — GitHub Issues

Use this before finalizing any issue-triage or backlog output.

## 1. Classification
- Is the ticket correctly identified as bug, feature request, support, duplicate, task, or split candidate?
- Did you separate the problem from the reporter’s proposed fix?

## 2. Evidence and impact
- Is the observed behavior clear?
- Is evidence quality stated honestly?
- Did you consider who is affected, frequency, workaround availability, and business or trust risk?

## 3. Prioritization quality
- Is severity or priority justified rather than implied?
- Did you avoid urgency inflation?
- Is the recommended next action proportionate to the evidence and impact?

## 4. Execution readiness
- Would the next owner know what to do from this issue?
- Are title, problem statement, repro details, expected behavior, evidence, and acceptance criteria clear where relevant?
- If the issue is not actionable yet, is that explicit?

## 5. Queue hygiene
- Did you look for duplicate, split, close, or escalation opportunities?
- Did you recommend label, form, or workflow improvements when recurring patterns appeared?
- Did you avoid preserving backlog noise just because it exists already?
