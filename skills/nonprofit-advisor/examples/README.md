# Nonprofit Advisor — Example Output Shapes

Use these as patterns for mission-led nonprofit recommendations.

## Example 1 — Revenue strategy recommendation

**Input:**
A youth-mentoring nonprofit has $250k annual revenue, a founder-led board, and wants to reach $1M without losing mission focus.

**Good output shape:**
| Revenue path | Fit now | Why | Main risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Major donors | High | story and founder access are strong | overreliance on a few relationships |
| Grants | Medium | program is legible but impact evidence is still thin | time-heavy reporting burden |
| Membership | Low | weak recurring community identity today | distraction from core build |
| Earned revenue | Low-Medium | possible training product later | mission drift if overbuilt too early |

Then add:
- recommended sequence for next 12 months
- capabilities to build first
- board role in fundraising
- metrics that show healthier revenue quality

## Example 2 — Governance cleanup

**Input:**
Our board keeps getting involved in staff-level decisions and meetings are chaotic.

**Good output shape:**
- Diagnosis:
  - reserved board decisions are unclear
  - committee structure does not match actual work
  - reporting mixes oversight and operating minutiae
- Fixes:
  1. define board-only decisions
  2. create a consent agenda for routine items
  3. move staff execution metrics to a dashboard appendix
  4. assign a governance committee owner
- Deliverables:
  - board/staff responsibility matrix
  - new board meeting agenda structure
  - 90-day governance reset plan

## Example 3 — Program portfolio review

**Input:**
We run four community programs but may only have capacity for two next year.

**Good output shape:**
| Program | Mission contribution | Evidence strength | Funding fit | Team capacity fit | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mentoring | High | Medium-High | Strong | Strong | Expand |
| Events | Medium | Low | Mixed | Weak | Reduce |
| Resource hotline | High | Medium | Medium | Medium | Maintain |
| Advocacy workshops | Medium | Low | Weak | Weak | Pause |

Then add:
- what evidence would change the call
- donor communication implications
- transition risks if a program is paused

## Example 4 — Case-for-support structure

**Input:**
Help us explain our impact better to potential donors.

**Good output shape:**
- Problem: what beneficiaries face now
- Why current alternatives fall short
- Our approach: what the nonprofit does differently
- Proof: outputs, outcomes, and honest limits
- Why now: urgency and opportunity
- Use of funds: what the gift unlocks
- Credibility: leadership, partners, and execution track record
