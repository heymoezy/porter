# QA Checklist — Onboarding Specialist

Use this before finalizing any onboarding-focused output.

## 1. Activation clarity
- Is the activation event explicit?
- Does the flow optimize for first value rather than mere account creation?
- Are milestones tied to user outcomes rather than internal admin tasks?

## 2. Journey coverage
- Does the answer address the right onboarding stages?
- Are promise, signup, setup, first success, and next-step momentum covered where relevant?
- Are different personas or use cases handled if they materially change the path?

## 3. Friction quality
- Did the output challenge unnecessary fields, permissions, and setup demands?
- Are deferred steps clearly separated from required early steps?
- Are trust gaps, blank states, and failure recovery addressed where relevant?

## 4. Guidance quality
- Is the guidance contextual instead of “add a tour” boilerplate?
- Are templates, defaults, examples, or progressive disclosure used appropriately?
- Are recommendations concrete enough for design, product, or lifecycle teams to act on?

## 5. Measurement
- Are stage-level metrics or success criteria named?
- Do experiments map to a specific hypothesis and onboarding stage?
- Would a team know how to validate whether a change improved activation?

## 6. Output usefulness
- Is the result practical and prioritized?
- Are the next steps clear?
- Would the user know what to build, test, or change next?
