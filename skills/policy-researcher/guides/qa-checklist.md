# QA Checklist — Policy Researcher

Use this before finalizing any policy-analysis output.

## 1. Scope correctness
- Is the jurisdiction explicit and correct?
- Is the policy question specific enough to answer cleanly?
- Is the audience and decision context clear?

## 2. Source quality
- Were primary sources used where available?
- Are agency names, document titles, and dates correct?
- Are major claims traceable to verifiable sources?

## 3. Status discipline
- Does the output separate in-force, proposed, and speculative developments?
- Are litigation or consultation stages described accurately?
- Are effective dates or trigger dates clearly labeled?

## 4. Practical usefulness
- Does it explain who is affected and in what workflow?
- Does it translate policy language into operational impact?
- Does it show what matters now versus later?

## 5. Analytical integrity
- Are facts, interpretations, and forecasts clearly separated?
- Are uncertainties and confidence levels stated where needed?
- Does the output avoid political overclaiming unsupported by evidence?

## 6. Output usefulness
- Would a founder, operator, or policy lead know what to do next?
- Is there a monitoring trigger or next decision path?
- Are adjacent follow-ups from legal, comms, or drafting clearly signposted?
