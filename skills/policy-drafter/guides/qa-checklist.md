# QA Checklist — Policy Drafter

Use this before finalizing any policy-writing output.

## 1. Purpose, authority, and scope
- Is it obvious why the policy exists?
- Is the source authority or requirement clear?
- Are scope boundaries and exclusions explicit?

## 2. Rule clarity
- Are obligations and prohibitions written with intentional modal verbs?
- Can the intended audience understand the rules on first read?
- Are vague or aspirational statements reduced or removed?

## 3. Governance completeness
- Is there a named owner or accountable role?
- Are responsibilities, approvals, exceptions, and review cadence covered?
- Are recordkeeping or evidence expectations defined when needed?

## 4. Policy vs procedure discipline
- Does the document state governing rules rather than burying itself in step-by-step instructions?
- Are operational details included only where they aid enforcement or interpretation?
- Would a separate SOP still have room to exist cleanly?

## 5. Edge cases and enforceability
- Are conflict, exception, or emergency scenarios addressed where relevant?
- Would different managers interpret the same rule the same way?
- Does the output avoid implying legal sign-off?

## 6. Output usefulness
- Could the owner adopt or circulate this draft with minimal cleanup?
- Are assumptions and unresolved questions called out?
- Is the result auditable, maintainable, and readable?
