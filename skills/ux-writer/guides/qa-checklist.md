# QA Checklist — ux-writer

- The user moment, UI context, and intended action are explicit.
- The work is clearly interface microcopy, not marketing or layout design.
- Labels and messages are concise without becoming vague or misleading.
- Errors explain what happened, what it means, and what to do next when possible.
- Warnings and confirmations make consequences clear.
- Empty, loading, success, and edge states are covered where relevant.
- Terminology and action language stay consistent across related strings.
- Localization, truncation, and plain-language accessibility concerns are considered.
- Recommendations are implementation-ready and note any context gaps that could change the wording.
- The result is concise, structured, and free of filler.
