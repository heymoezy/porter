# QA Checklist — Approval Governor

Use this before finalizing any approval-governance output.

## 1. Action clarity
- Is the exact proposed action stated clearly?
- Is the affected scope named explicitly?
- Is it clear what would change if the action proceeds?

## 2. Authority check
- Did the output check whether approval already exists?
- Is the decision maker or approving authority identified when relevant?
- Does the output avoid claiming prior approval covers a different action?

## 3. Risk assessment
- Are impact, reversibility, blast radius, and sensitivity considered?
- Are key assumptions or unknowns flagged?
- Is the recommendation proportional to the actual risk?

## 4. Decision quality
- Does the output clearly say proceed, require approval, escalate, or deny?
- Is the reason for that decision understandable in one read?
- Are conditions or exclusions explicit?

## 5. Operational usefulness
- If approval is needed, is the ask concise and approvable?
- If the action is too risky, is a narrower alternative proposed?
- Would the output prevent accidental overreach or misinterpretation?

## 6. Tone and discipline
- Is the result decisive without sounding bureaucratic?
- Is the language concise and concrete?
- Does the output avoid policy theater and generic caution?
